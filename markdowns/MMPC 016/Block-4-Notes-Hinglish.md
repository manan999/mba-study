### UNIT 10: INTERNATIONAL MARKETING

#### 1. Domestic vs. International Marketing (Domestic aur International Marketing me diff)
*   **Concept:** Dono me basic marketing goals same hote hain (consumer demand satisfy karna), par conditions, cross-border regulations, aur socio-cultural variations international marketing ko different aur difficult banate hain.
*   **Key Differences:**
    | Feature | Domestic Marketing | International Marketing |
    | :--- | :--- | :--- |
    | **Markets** | Ek single familiar market segment. | Multiple diverse markets (different culture, language, laws). |
    | **Control & Research** | Aasan control; market awareness default hoti hai. | Complex environment; detail cross-cultural research mandatory hai. |
    | **Product & Brand** | Standard product mix directly launch ho sakta hai. | local norms ke hisab se product changes/adaptation zaroori hain. |
    | **Media & Promotion** | Known local media systems aur common message channels. | Diverse media laws aur variable advertising guidelines. |
*   **International Marketing Mix:** Deciding between **Standardization** (seeling the same product/ad globally to save costs and maintain brand consistency) and **Adaptation** (modifying product, price, place, and ads to suit local cultural, legal, and economic conditions).

#### 2. Key Drivers and Challenges (Drivers aur challenges)
*   **Drivers:** National economies liberalization, tax holidays (low tax zones), trade agreements (RTAs/FTAs), domestic market saturation, high R&D cost optimization, and logistical improvements.
*   **Challenges:** Rapid technological change (Industry 4.0), local competitors ka strong presence (local store brands), consumer empowerment (demanding custom values), and high cultural variations.

#### 3. Market Selection, Channels, and Exporting Steps (Market select karna aur export ke steps)
*   **International Market Selection:** Filtering processes ke through evaluate hota hai:
    1.  *Macro-Screening:* GDP growth, currency stability, aur political risk filter out karna.
    2.  *Micro-Screening:* Competitor setup, local demand depth, aur distribution channel capabilities analyze karna.
    3.  *Final Selection:* Strategic feasibility check karna.
*   **Distribution Channels:**
    *   *Direct Channels:* Clients ko directly sale karna (e.g., self-owned stores, websites). High control, high cost.
    *   *Indirect Channels:* Intermediaries (distributors, local agents) use karna. Low cost aur fast access, but low control over customer interface.
*   **Steps for Successful Exporting:**
    1.  *Market Research & Product Customization* (local standards check karna).
    2.  *Export licenses and Registrations* (e.g., India me Import Export Code - IEC).
    3.  *Buyers identification & secure order agreements* (via trade fairs or agents).
    4.  *Pre & post shipment export finance arrangement*.
    5.  *Manufacturing, packaging, and quality checks*.
    6.  *Customs clearance, shipping logistics, and submitting documents for bank payment*.

#### 4. Market Research & Consumer Behaviour (Market research aur customer behavior)
*   **Significance of Market Research:** Cross-border business me failures ko control karne ke liye research core tool hai. Local rules, customers behavior, aur competitors behavior scan karta hai.
*   **International Consumer Behaviour:** Economic variables ke sath cultural values, local aesthetic sensibilities, religion, aur social status customer buying decisions shape karte hain (e.g., different regions me skin tones aur food habits ke hisab se Barbie dolls aur food menu modify karna).

### UNIT 11: INTERNATIONAL FINANCE

#### 1. The Global Financial System & Foreign Exchange Market
*   **Global Financial System:** Cross-border capital flows aur trade calculations ko control karne wale international institutions, rules, and structures (IMF, World Bank, Forex Markets) ka system. Isme currency values ka change and interest fluctuations MNCs profits ko direct hit karte hain.
*   **Foreign Exchange (Forex) Market:** currencies trading ka decentralized global market.
    *   *Participants:* Commercial banks, central banks, MNCs, speculators.
    *   *Exposures (Currency Risk):*
        1.  *Transaction Exposure:* Contract sign hone se lekar settlement hone ke beech currency value badalne ka risk.
        2.  *Translation Exposure:* Foreign subsidiaries ke balance sheets ko parent currency me translate karte waqt accounting value loss hone ka risk.
        3.  *Economic Exposure:* Exchange rate changes ki wajah se long-term global competitiveness aur operational cash flows affect hone ka risk.

#### 2. Balance of Payments (BoP)
Ek specific financial period me country ke residents aur baaki world ke beech hue transactions ka accounting record:
1.  **Trade Account:** Merchandised (visible) goods ka export and import value calculation.
2.  **Current Account:** Services (invisible) trade, remittance transfers (money sent by workers), aur dividend/interest income.
3.  **Capital Account:** Capital assets transfers aur patent/trademark transactions.
4.  **Financial/Official Reserves Account:** FDI/FPI investments and borrowing details, and central bank ke gold/foreign currency reserves changes.

#### 3. Terms of Payment in International Trade (Payment methods)
*   **Cash in Advance (Payment in Advance):** Importer dispatch se pehle hi pay kar deta hai. Exporter ke liye 100% safe; importer ke liye high-risk.
*   **Documentary Collection:** Shipping papers bank to bank route hote hain. Importer tabhi papers clear kar sakta hai jab:
    *   *Documents Against Payment (D/P) / Sight Draft:* Importer documents milte hi payment kare.
    *   *Documents Against Acceptance (D/A) / Usance Draft:* Importer signed commitment de ki woh 30-90 din me pay karega (credit terms).
*   **Open Account:** Exporter direct cargo aur documents clear karwa deta hai. Importer credit cycle (e.g., 60 days) ke baad pay karta hai. Exporter ke liye maximum risk method.
*   **Letter of Credit (L/C):** Importer ka bank (issuing bank) guarantee leta hai ki documents matching rules submit karne par bank exporter ko directly pay karega. Yeh bank contract hai jo **UCPDC 600** ke rules follow karta hai.

#### 4. Foreign Investment & Risk Mitigation (Investments aur hedging)
*   **FDI vs. FPI:**
    *   *Foreign Direct Investment (FDI):* Foreign companies ke physical assets me direct investment (Greenfield, JVs, Acquisitions) with long-term control.
    *   *Foreign Portfolio Investment (FPI):* Stock market, bonds, GDRs/ADRs me investments without direct control. Liquid and volatile investment.
*   **Risk Mitigation & Hedging:**
    *   *Hedging:* Derivatives instruments use karke financial loss block karne ka process.
    *   *Derivatives:* **Forward Contracts** (future currency price lock karna), **Futures Contracts** (exchange-traded standardized contracts), **Currency Options** (currency buy/sell karne ka right, obligation nahi), aur **Currency Swaps**.
    *   *Approaches:* **Full Hedging** (sari exposure secure karna), **Selective Hedging** (high risk secure karna), or **Speculation** (unhedged rehna to profit from fluctuations).

### UNIT 12: INTERNATIONAL OPERATIONS & LOGISTICS MANAGEMENT

#### 1. Strategic Issues in International Operations Management
Operations coordination in MNEs key decisions:
*   **Plant Location:** Factory setup location select karna based on labor cost, raw material availability, government subsidies, and infrastructure.
*   **Capacity Planning:** Deciding centralized global hub (high scale benefits) versus decentralized regional plants (custom options and low logistics cost).
*   **Sourcing & Make-or-Buy:** Components khud banana (high IP security) versus global suppliers se outsource karna (low cost benefits).

#### 2. Logistics & Marketing Interface (Logistics aur marketing ka interface)
*   **International Logistics Management:** Materials aur cargo ka cross-border movement aur storage manage karna.
*   **Marketing Interface:** Logistics marketing promises ko fulfillment level par lane ka core physical machine hai.
*   *Coordination Areas:*
    1.  *Customer Service standards:* Delivery speed ko optimization points ke sath map karna.
    2.  *Pricing and Packaging:* Design aisi ho jo long-distance transport face kare aur customers ko attract kare.
    3.  *Inventory Management:* Out of stock checks and storage costs control.

#### 3. Supply Chain Management (SCM) & Modelling
*   **SCM Parts:**
    *   *Upstream:* Raw material procurement and supplier logistics.
    *   *Horizontal:* Internal factory production flows, materials handling, and QA.
    *   *Downstream:* Distribution networks to reach end customers.
*   **SCM Models:**
    *   **SCOR (Supply Chain Operations Reference) Model:** Council design framework to measure end-to-end reliability, agility, costs, and assets performance.
    *   **GSCF (Global Supply Chain Forum) Model:** Cross-functional management setup involving R&D, sales, finance, and logistics teams.

### UNIT 13: INTERNATIONAL HUMAN RESOURCE MANAGEMENT (IHRM)

#### 1. Scope, Staffing Approaches, and Cross-Cultural Issues
*   **IHRM Scope:** Managing Parent Country Nationals (PCNs), Host Country Nationals (HCNs), and Third Country Nationals (TCNs) globally.
*   **Staffing Approaches (EPRG Framework):**
    1.  *Ethnocentric:* Key positions are filled by Parent Country Nationals (PCNs/Expatriates). Home practices best mani jati hain.
    2.  *Polycentric:* Subsidiary management Host Country Nationals (HCNs) ke control me hoti hai. Assumes locals market ko best handle karte hain.
    3.  *Regiocentric:* Staffing regions basis par split (e.g., Asian staff transfer within Asia only).
    4.  *Geocentric:* Sahi skills wale manager ko post dena, nationality matters nahi karti.
*   **Cross-Cultural Issues:** Language differences, working ethics, and different behaviors check karna and adjust karna to avoid conflicts.

#### 2. Expatriate Selection, Training, and Performance Appraisal (Expatriates management)
*   **Selection factors:** Technical abilities, emotional intelligence, cultural flexibility, and family adaptability.
*   **Expatriate Training:** Culture shock control karne ke liye training:
    *   *Pre-Departure Training:* Cultural orientations, area studies, language learning, sensitivity tests, and site visits.
    *   *On-the-job training in host country*.
*   **Appraisal Challenges:**
    *   *Geographical Distance:* Daily work monitors not possible.
    *   *Evaluator Bias:* Conflict between home country evaluators (far away, knows core goals) and host country evaluators (local peers, knows daily behavior).
    *   *Solution:* Use multi-rater frameworks (360 feedback), customized metrics, and local environment adjustment allowances.

#### 3. International Compensation (International salary package)
*   **Objectives:** Recruit candidates globally, easy relocation transitions, and subsidiary equity.
*   **Two Main Approaches:**
    1.  **The Going-Rate (Market Rate) Approach:** Host country base salary structure ke scale par compensate karna.
        *   *Pros:* Local peers ke sath equivalence maintain rehta hai.
        *   *Cons:* Low salary country me relocation hard ho jata hai; repatriation issues.
    2.  **The Balance Sheet Approach:** Expatriate's home country standard of living maintain karna, with expatriate premium/incentives.
        *   *Pros:* Simplifies repatriation; home country parity remains.
        *   *Cons:* Same location me different nationalities ke beech wide disparities create ho sakti hain.
