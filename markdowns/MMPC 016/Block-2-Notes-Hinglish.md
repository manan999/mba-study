### UNIT 4: TRADE THEORIES & FDI APPROACHES

#### 1. Importance and Classification of Trade Theories (Trade Theories ki importance aur classification)
*   **Why Study Trade Theories?** Yeh theories batati hain ki international trade kaise chalta hai, aur governments ko efficient trade policies banane me help karti hain. Companies isse location-specific aur comparative advantages ko identify karti hain.
*   **Classification:**
    1.  **Classical/Country-Based Theories:** Country-level factors par focus karti hain (Mercantilism, Absolute Advantage, Comparative Advantage, Opportunity Cost, Reciprocal Demand).
    2.  **Modern/Firm-Based Theories:** Factor endowments aur company-level advantages par focus karti hain (Heckscher-Ohlin Theory, Product Life Cycle, Global Strategic Rivalry).
    3.  **FDI/MNC-Based Theories:** Yeh samjhati hain ki companies export ya licensing karne ki jagah foreign countries me directly invest (FDI) kyun karti hain (Market Imperfections, Eclectic Paradigm, Internalization).

#### 2. Classical Trade Theories at a Glance
*   **Mercantilism (16th-18th Century):** Is theory ke mutabik desh ki wealth aur power uske gold aur silver reserves se measure hoti hai. Yeh protectionism ko support karti hai (exports ko maximize karna aur imports ko minimize karna). Yeh ek **zero-sum game** hai (ek desh ka gain matlab doosre desh ka loss).
    *   *Criticism:* Gold reserves badhne se domestic market me inflation (mehangai) badh jati hai, jisse exports uncompetitive ho jate hain. Yeh consumer welfare ko ignore karta hai.
*   **Absolute Cost Advantage (Adam Smith, 1776):** Agar koi country kisi product ko doosre desh se zyada efficiently (kam resources/labor me) bana sakti hai, toh use absolute advantage hai. Use wahi product export karna chahiye, aur jisme disadvantage ho use import karna chahiye.
    *   *Assumptions:* Labor hi production ka single factor hai; labor domestic boundaries me mobile hai par international boundaries me immobile hai; free trade rules hain.
*   **Comparative Cost Advantage (David Ricardo, 1817):** Agar ek country *dono* products banane me absolute advantage rakhti hai, tab bhi trade beneficial ho sakta hai. Country ko us product par focus karna chahiye jisme uski relative efficiency highest ho (comparative advantage), aur us product ko import karna chahiye jisme disadvantage relative lowest ho (comparative disadvantage).
*   **Haberler's Opportunity Cost Theory (1936):** Ricardo ki labor-only limit ko dur kiya. Isne bataya ki kisi product ki cost matlab doosre product ka woh sacrifice jo extra unit banane ke liye kiya gaya. Isse **Production Possibility Curve (PPC)** ke through explain kiya jata hai.
*   **Mill's Theory of Reciprocal Demand (1921):** Do deshon ke beech actual terms of trade (exchange ratio) unki ek doosre ke products ke liye demand ki strength aur elasticity par depend karti hai.

#### 3. Heckscher-Ohlin (H-O) Theory & The Leontief Paradox
*   **H-O Theory (Factor Endowment Theory):** Comparative advantage factor endowments (resources ki availability) se decide hota hai. Jo resources desh me abundant (saste aur zyada) hain, unhe use karke banaye gaye products ko country export karegi (e.g., labor-abundant country like India labor-intensive products export karegi), aur scarce resources wale products import karegi.
*   **The Leontief Paradox:** 
    *   *The Study:* Wassily Leontief (1947) ne US data par H-O model test kiya.
    *   *The Paradox (Virodh):* US ek capital-rich country hai, lekin Leontief ne paya ki US ke exports labor-intensive the aur imports capital-intensive the, jo H-O theory ke bilkul opposite tha.
    *   *Explanations to Resolve the Paradox:*
        1.  **US Tariff Policy:** Tariffs ne labor-intensive domestic industries ko protect kiya, jisse labor-intensive imports kam ho gaye.
        2.  **Human Capital Investment:** US labor highly skilled aur educated thi, isliye unka "labor" output capital-equivalent tha.
        3.  **Natural Resource Imports:** US heavy natural resources import karta tha jinhe extract karne me huge capital lagta tha, jisse data skewed ho gaya.

#### 4. Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Theories
*   **Stephen Hymer's Market Imperfections Approach (1976):** Classical theories perfect competition manti thin jisme FDI bekaar lagta tha (kyunki local firms ko home advantage hota hai). Hymer ne kaha ki MNEs isliye exist karti hain kyunki markets perfect nahi hote. Companies ke paas *ownership advantages* (monopolistic power, brand value, patented technology, access to capital) hote hain jo foreign operations ke extra risks ko cover karte hain.
*   **Vernon's Product Life Cycle (PLC) Theory:** Products teen stages se guzarte hain:
    1.  *New Product Stage:* Innovation aur production pehle home country (high-income market) me hota hai.
    2.  *Maturing Product Stage:* Product standardized hota hai aur exports badhte hain. Competition aane par companies developed host countries me subsidiaries set karti hain.
    3.  *Standardized Product Stage:* Production completely standard ho jata hai. Cost cut karne ke liye manufacturing low-wage developing countries me shift ho jati hai, aur home country wahan se import karne lagta hai.
*   **Dunning's Eclectic Paradigm (The OLI Model):** FDI tabhi hoga jab teeno advantages ek sath milenge:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                     THE OLI ADVANTAGE TRIAD                     │
├───────────────────────┬───────────────────────┬─────────────────┤
│     Ownership (O)     │     Location (L)      │Internalization(I)│
│ • Firm-specific assets│ • Host country ke     │ • Licensing ke  │
│   jaise patents, tech,│   factors: sasta      │   mukable firm  │
│   brand value, aur    │   labor, resources,   │   ke andar work │
│   management skills.  │   markets, incentives.│   karna safe hai.│
└───────────────────────┴───────────────────────┴─────────────────┘
```

### UNIT 5: WTO AGREEMENTS

#### 1. Distinction between GATT and WTO (GATT aur WTO me kya farq hai?)
| Feature | GATT (General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade) | WTO (World Trade Organization) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nature** | Ek multilateral treaty thi, koi organization nahi thi. | Ek permanent international organization hai jiska legal status hai. |
| **Scope** | Sirf trade in goods (saman) ko cover karta tha. | Goods ke sath Services (GATS) aur Intellectual Property (TRIPS) ko bhi cover karta hai. |
| **Commitment** | Applied on a provisional (temporarily) basis. | Agreements full, permanent, aur binding hote hain ("Single Undertaking"). |
| **Dispute Settlement** | Slow aur non-automatic tha. Loser country ruling block kar sakti thi. | Fast aur automatic system hai. Isme strict Dispute Settlement Body hai. |
| **Secretariat** | Chota aur ad-hoc secretariat tha. | Geneva me bada aur permanent secretariat hai. |

#### 2. Key WTO Agreements & Business Implications (WTO ke main agreements)
*   **Agreement on Tariffs:** Tariffs ko bound (set limits) aur reduce karne par focus karta hai:
    *   *Most-Favoured-Nation (MFN) Tariffs:* Jo standard tariff rates sabhi WTO members ko bina discrimination ke diye jate hain.
    *   *Preferential Tariffs:* Lower tariff rates jo FTAs/RTAs ke under member states aapas me set karte hain.
*   **Agreement on Agriculture (AoA):** Agricultural trade ko fair aur market-oriented banane ke liye teen pillars pe work karta hai:
    *   *Market Access:* Non-tariff barriers ko tariff me badal kar unhe reduce karna.
    *   *Export Subsidies:* Un subsidies ko phase out karna jo export pricing ko artificially low karti hain.
    *   *Domestic Support (Subsidies classification):* Subsidies ko teen "Boxes" me rakha gaya hai:
        *   **Green Box:** Jo trade distort nahi karti (e.g., agriculture R&D, environment programmes, crop insurance). Inpar koi limit nahi hai.
        *   **Blue Box:** Production-limiting programmes ke under direct payments. Inpar minimal restrictions hain.
        *   **Amber Box:** Jo trade distort karti hain (e.g., power/fertilizer subsidies, price supports). Inhe limit aur reduce karna padta hai.
*   **TRIPS (Trade-Related Aspects of Intellectual Property Rights):** Patents, copyrights, trademarks, industrial designs, geographical indications (GI), aur trade secrets ko globally protect karne ke minimum standards set karta hai.
*   **TRIMS (Trade-Related Investment Measures):** Host countries ko aisi investment rules banane se rokta hai jo trade ko restrict karein (e.g., local components use karne ki compulsion ya trade balancing limits).
*   **SPS & TBT Measures:**
    *   *Sanitary & Phytosanitary (SPS):* Food safety aur plant/animal health guidelines.
    *   *Technical Barriers to Trade (TBT):* Ensuring technical regulations, packaging, and labels trade barriers na banein.

#### 3. GATS: Four Modes of Supply of Services (GATS ke under services trade ke 4 modes)
GATS services trade ko char alag tareeqon me define karta hai:
1.  **Mode 1: Cross-Border Supply:** Service border cross karti hai, par provider aur consumer dono apne deshon me hi rehte hain.
    *   *Example:* Ek Indian company ka email/internet ke through software coding ya business report USA client ko bhejna.
2.  **Mode 2: Consumption Abroad:** Consumer doosre desh me ja kar service consume karta hai.
    *   *Example:* Ek French citizen ka India me ghoomna (Tourism) ya kisi American student ka Oxford university me padhna (Education).
3.  **Mode 3: Commercial Presence:** Service provider host country me business/office setup karta hai.
    *   *Example:* Citibank ka India me branches open karna ya Subway ka Japan me outlets setup karna.
4.  **Mode 4: Presence of Natural Persons:** Ek individual temporarily doosre desh me ja kar service provide karta hai.
    *   *Example:* Ek Indian IT Consultant ka onsite project implementation ke liye US client office me 6 mahine ke liye jana.

### UNIT 6: REGIONAL TRADE BLOCS

#### 1. Levels/Types of Economic Integration (Economic Integration ke levels)
RTAs ke under countries aapas me trade preferences share karti hain. Iske integration levels sequential hote hain:

```
[Preferential Trade Agreement (PTA)] -> Selected goods par low tariffs apply karna.
   ↓
[Free Trade Area (FTA)] -> Internal trade me tariff free; external tariffs individual. (e.g., NAFTA).
   ↓
[Customs Union (CU)] -> FTA features + Non-members ke liye Common External Tariff (CET).
   ↓
[Common Market] -> Customs Union + Labor and capital ki free movement.
   ↓
[Economic Union] -> Common Market + Harmonized fiscal/monetary policies, common currency (e.g., EU).
   ↓
[Political Union] -> Single political governance, foreign policy, and merged status.
```

*   **Rules of Origin (RoO):** FTAs me bohot important hai. Yeh check karta hai ki non-member countries FTA ka use karke backdoor entry na karein (saste route se goods send na karein).

#### 2. Benefits of Regional Trade Blocs (Trade Blocs ke fayde)
*   **Economies of Scale:** Bada market size companies ko large-scale production ka mauka deta hai, jisse unit costs reduce hoti hain.
*   **Production Efficiencies:** Comparative advantage ke base par resource utilization badhta hai, aur block ke andar high competition se innovation badhta.
*   **Employment & Growth:** Duty-free market size ki wajah se FDI badhta hai, jobs create hoti hain, aur real incomes badhne se economic growth milti hai.
*   **Trade Creation vs. Trade Diversion:**
    *   *Trade Creation:* High-cost domestic production ki jagah member country se saste imports aana (welfare-enhancing).
    *   *Trade Diversion:* Low-cost non-member imports ki jagah member country se preferential high-cost imports aana (welfare-reducing).

#### 3. Role of the European Union (EU) in International Business
*   **The Model Union:** EU duniya ka sabse integrated trade bloc (Economic Union) hai.
*   **Key Contributions:**
    *   *Common Currency (Euro):* 20 eurozone countries me exchange risk aur transaction fees ko khatam karta hai.
    *   *Single Market:* 450+ million wealthy consumers ka borderless market.
    *   *Standard Setter:* EU ke strict regulations (e.g., data privacy ke liye GDPR, chemicals ke liye REACH) ab global MNCs ke liye default standards ban chuke hain.
