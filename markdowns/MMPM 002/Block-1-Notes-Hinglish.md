### UNIT 1: SALES MANAGEMENT: ROLE, NATURE AND ETHICS

#### 1. Nature and Scope of Sales Management
*   **Definition:** Sales Management ek business unit ke personal selling activities ki planning, direction aur control hai. Isme sales force ko recruit karna, select karna, train karna, equip karna, assign karna, route planning karna, supervise karna, compensation pay karna, motivate karna aur evaluate karna shamil hai.
*   **Nature:**
    *   *Integration with Marketing:* Personal selling marketing mix ke Promotion element ka ek implementation arm hai. Sales planning ko overall marketing strategy ke saath align hona chahiye.
    *   *Revenue Generation:* Baki saare departments (jaise HR, Admin) cost centers hote hain, lekin sales department company ka primary direct revenue-generating division hota hai.
*   **Three Levels of Sales Managers (Sales Managers ke 3 Levels):**
    1.  *Top-Level:* Strategic planning, policy formulation aur sales budgeting par focus karte hain.
    2.  *Middle-Level:* Tactical implementation, regional sales goals aur monitoring par focus karte hain.
    3.  *First-Level (Field/Line Managers):* Daily supervision, territory management aur sales force ki direct coaching par focus karte hain.

#### 2. Evolution of Sales Management (Sales Management ka Itihaas)
*   **Pre-Industrial Era:** Production bohot chote scale par aur local level par hota tha. Main challenge supply ka tha, demand ka nahi. Ek single owner hi production aur sales dono manage karta tha.
*   **Industrial Revolution:** Mass production ki wajah se surplus goods (zyada maal) banne lage. Naye geographical markets ko dhoondne aur wahan expand karne ke liye specialized sales departments banaye gaye.
*   **Middlemen Era:** Distribution channels (wholesalers, retailers) ke badhne ki wajah se manufacturer aur consumer ka direct contact mushkil ho gaya.
*   **Modern Strategic Era:** Sales management ab sirf normal selling se aage badhkar strategic planning, sales forecasting, customer relationship management (CRM), territory design aur information communication technology (ICT) integration tak pahunch gaya hai.

```
[Pre-Industrial: Localized/Simple] ──► [Post-Industrial: Market Expansion] ──► [Modern: Strategic/ICT Driven]
```

#### 3. Role of Sales Function in Marketing (Selling vs. Marketing)
*   **Selling vs. Marketing me Antar:**
| Parameter | Selling (Vikray) | Marketing (Vipanan) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Focus** | Product-focused (Seller ki zaroorat). | Customer-focused (Customer ki needs/wants). |
| **Philosophy** | "Product ke badle cash lo." | "Value-satisfying goods banao." |
| **Horizon** | Short-term volume aur transaction-oriented. | Long-term relationship aur brand loyalty. |
| **Sequence** | Production khatam hone ke baad shuru hota hai. | Production se pehle shuru hota hai (needs assessment) aur sale ke baad bhi chalta hai. |

#### 4. Managing Ethics in Sales Environment (Ethical Climate)
*   **Common Sales Ethical Vulnerabilities (Galtiyan):** Bribes/kickbacks, puffery (exaggerated claims ya jhooti tareef), moonlighting (ek saath do jagah kaam karna), expense accounts me hera-pheri karna, product safety info chhupana aur competitor ke secrets nikalne ke liye customers ka use karna.
*   **Ethical Work Climate Dimensions (Ethical Climate ke 4 Aspects):**
    1.  *Policies and Rules:* Formally documented aur clear ethical codes of conduct.
    2.  *Trust and Responsibility:* Salespersons ko bina excessive micromanagement ke responsibly act karne ke liye empower karna.
    3.  *Peer Behavior:* Coworkers kis hadd tak high moral standards follow karte hain.
    4.  *Sales Emphasis:* Profit pressure aur ethical behavior ke beech ka balance. Quotas meet karne ka heavy pressure aur strict controls ethical climate ko kharab karte hain.

### UNIT 2: DIVERSITY OF SELLING SITUATIONS

#### 1. Growing Importance of Personal Selling (Indian Context)
*   **Personal Selling:** Paid representatives aur prospects ke beech direct, two-way communication jo transaction ko close karne me help kare.
*   **Key Drivers in the Indian Market (India me iska importance kyun hai?):**
    *   *Product Complexity:* Technical/appliances sales (jaise water purifiers, solar panels) ka badhna jisme product demonstration ki zaroorat hoti hai.
    *   *Low Literacy / Consumer Education:* Rural/semi-urban markets me personal selling ek educational tool ki tarah kaam karta hai.
    *   *High marginal value of Rupee:* Indian consumers paise ki poori value (value for money) chahte hain, iske liye face-to-face trust building zaroori hai.
    *   *Affordability:* Choti firms jo massive advertising budgets afford nahi kar sakti hain, woh targeted personal selling par rely karti hain.

#### 2. Essential Qualities and Skills of a Successful Salesperson
*   **Key Traits (Kotler ke according 2 main qualities):**
    *   *Empathy:* Customer ke shoes me step in karne aur unki actual needs/feelings ko samajhne ki ability.
    *   *Ego Drive / Persuasion:* Sale close karne ki andaruni zaroorat (sirf paise ke liye nahi, balki self-actualization aur personal jeet ke liye).
*   **Other Qualities:** Clean professional appearance, curiosity (desire to learn), clear speech/tone, quantitative/writing ability (reports likhne ke liye), self-confidence, integrity aur self-motivation.

#### 3. Classifying Selling Situations and Roles
*   **Service Selling:** Purane customers se repeat orders lena jinki buying habits hum pehle se jaante hain.
*   **Developmental Selling:** Naye customers banana un prospects se jo pehle competitors ka product use kar rahe hain.
*   **Specific Roles (Sales ke Roles):**
    1.  *Delivery Salesperson:* Main kaam physical delivery ka hota hai (jaise milk/bread delivery); selling secondary hai.
    2.  *Inside Order Taker:* Retail counter assistant (jaise mall ke salesperson) jahan customer khud buy karne ki intent se aata hai.
    3.  *Outside Order Taker:* Regular retailers ke paas jaakar orders lena (jaise FMCG sales reps); isme hard selling nahi hoti.
    4.  *Missionary Salesperson:* Order creators jo direct order nahi lete balki goodwill build karte hain aur educate karte hain (jaise Pharmaceutical companies ke Medical Representatives / MRs).
    5.  *Sales Engineer / Technical Sales:* High-tech products (jaise factory machinery) bechna jiske liye deep technical knowledge chahiye.
    6.  *Salesperson of Non-Competing Companies:* Aise salespersons jo aapas me share karte hain leads (jaise steel bechne wala cement bechne wale ko lead de).
    7.  *Industrial Selling (B2B):* Raw materials, components, ya heavy machinery ko dusri businesses, government ya re-sellers ko bechna. Isme high transaction value, rational decision-making aur multiple buying influencers shamil hote hain.

### UNIT 3: THEORIES OF SELLING AND SELLING PROCESS

#### 1. Comparison of Theories of Selling (Selling ki Theories)
*   **AIDAS Theory (Seller-Oriented):** Yeh assume karta hai ki buyer ka dimaag paanch sequential stages se guzarta hai:
    *   *Attention (Dhyan):* Customer ke routine mind blocks ko break karna.
    *   *Interest (Ruchi):* Visual aids, brochures, ya demo ka use karke customer ko engaged rakhna.
    *   *Desire (Ichha):* Product ke features ko benefits me badalna taaki customer ke motivations se match ho sake.
    *   *Action:* Customer ki hesitation ko door karke deal close karna.
    *   *Satisfaction:* Purchase ke baad reassurance aur service dena taaki customer satisfied rahe aur cognitive dissonance na ho.
*   **Right Set of Circumstances Theory (Situation-Oriented):** *"Everything was right for the sale."* Isme salesperson external factors ko control karke aisi situation banata hai jahan customer naturally buy karne ke liye ready ho jata hai.
*   **Buying Formula Theory (Buyer-Oriented):** Yeh buyer ke internal mental sequence par focus karta hai. Iska path hai:
$$\text{Need} \longrightarrow \text{Solution} \longrightarrow \text{Purchase} \longrightarrow \text{Satisfaction}$$
    *   Salesperson ko link banana hota hai: Need $\to$ Product/Service $\to$ Brand $\to$ Purchase.
*   **Behavioral Equation Theory (Learning-Oriented):** J. Howard ka formula:
$$B = P \times D \times K \times V$$
    *   Jahan $B$ = Brand purchase response, $P$ = Predisposition/Force of habit, $D$ = Drive/Motivation level, $K$ = Incentive potential (satisfaction value), $V$ = Cue intensity (sales presentation ki quality). Kyunki variables multiplicative hain, agar koi bhi variable (jaise Motivation $D$ ya satisfaction $K$) 0 hoga, toh sale nahi hogi.

#### 2. The Personal Selling Process (Personal Selling ke Steps)
```
[Prospecting & Qualifying] ──► [Pre-approach] ──► [Approach] ──► [Presentation & Demo] 
                                                                         │
[Follow-up & Service] ◄── [Closing the Sale] ◄── [Objection Handling] ◄─┘
```
1.  **Prospecting and Qualifying:** Naye leads ko dhoondna (cold calling ya referral ke through) aur unhe qualify karna using **NAME** formula (Need, Authority, Money, Eligibility).
2.  **Pre-approach (The Planning Stage):** Prospect ke baare me study karna (buying procedures, current products, budget). Key steps:
    *   *Seeding:* Prospect ko call ke liye prepare karna.
    *   *Objective setting:* Call ka goal decide karna (appointment lena ya direct sale).
    *   *Strategy choice:* Presentation style aur preparation.
3.  **Approaching the Prospect:** Ek positive first impression banana. Strategies:
    *   *Non-product related:* Kisi mutual acquaintance (jaankaar) ka reference dena, self-introduction, free gift dena.
    *   *Piquing interest:* Customer benefit statement dena, curious karne wale statements bolna.
    *   *Product-related:* Direct product demonstration shuru karna ya model/sample dikhana.
4.  **Sales Presentation and Demonstration:**
    *   *Attention Probes:* Customer ke needs ko dhoondne ke liye **Open Probes** (jisme detailed answers milein) ka use karna na ki **Closed Probes** (yes/no wale).
    *   *Translation:* Product ke **features** (attributes, e.g., aluminum engine) ko customer ke **benefits** (fayde, e.g., higher mileage) me translate karna.
5.  **Negotiating Sales Resistance (Objection Handling):**
    *   *Sincere Objections:* Real problems (e.g., "no budget right now", guarantee claims).
    *   *Insincere Objections:* Faltu excuses (e.g., product ka packaging color accha nahi hai).
    *   *Objection Handling System:* **Listen** (sunna) $\to$ **Ask** (clarify karna) $\to$ **Respond** (finance offers, facts) $\to$ **Confirm** ("kya aap satisfied hain?") $\to$ **Move** to close.
6.  **Closing the Sale (Sale Close Karna):** Decision-making hesitation ko overcome karna. Techniques:
    *   *Alternative Close:* Option dena ("Main 1 box bhejoon ya 2 boxes?").
    *   *Summary Close:* Saare benefits ko summarize karke order maangna.
    *   *Picture Close:* Brand image ko creatively explain karna.
    *   *Closing with Empathy:* Client ke point of view se align karke pitch karna.
7.  **Follow-up and Servicing:** Delivery ke baad contact rakhna taaki grievances (shikayatein) solve ho sakein, trust build ho aur repeat sales aayein.

#### 3. Consultative Selling
*   **Concept:** Ek highly collaborative approach jahan salesperson ek business consultant ki tarah act karta hai. Sirf product bechne ke badle, woh client ke team ke sath work karke unke strategic goals aur bottlenecks ko samajhta hai aur custom solution design karta hai.
*   **Example:** Ek IT software firm kisi manufacturing plant ko customized ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning) software bech rahi hai. Salesperson alag-alag departments (Finance, Production, HR) se milkar unke problems samajhta hai aur unke liye ek customized software pack design karta hai.
