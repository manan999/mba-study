5. Complaints  are  a  natural  consequence  of  any service  activity.  Mistakes  are  a  critical  part  of every service. Since services are generally performed  in  the  presence  of  customers,  errors are  bound  to  happen.  While  it  may  not  be possible  to  prevent  all  errors,  companies  can learn  to  recover  from  them.  'Service  recovery' refers to the action taken by the service provider in  response  to  a  service  failure.  To  illustrate, consider  a  rental  car  customer  who  arrives  to check in and finds that no automobile is available of the  size  reserved  and  the  price quoted.  In  an  effort  to  recover,  the  car  rental agent  immediately  upgrades  this  customer  to  a much  better  vehicle  at  the  original  price.  The customer, thrilled with this compensation, reports that she is extremely satisfied with this experience, is even more impressed with the firm than  she  was  before,  and  vows  to  be  loyal  into the future. Although such extreme instances are relatively  rare,  this  idea  -  that    an  initially disappointed customer who has experienced good service  recovery  might  be  even  more  satisfied and loyal as a result, is important.
