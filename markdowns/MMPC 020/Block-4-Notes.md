### UNIT 12: CSR REPORTING PROCESS & AUDITING

#### 1. Concept and Rationale of CSR Reporting
*   **Definition:** The process of communication by an organization about its social, environmental, and ethical performances to its stakeholders.
*   **Rationale:**
    1.  *Transparency:* Builds trust with consumers, investors, and communities.
    2.  *Legitimacy:* Demonstrates compliance with legal regulations (e.g., SEBI guidelines).
    3.  *Internal Improvement:* Helps the company identify operational inefficiencies and risks (e.g., resource waste).

#### 2. Tools and Techniques of CSR Reporting
*   **GRI (Global Reporting Initiative):** The most widely used international standard for sustainability reporting, focusing on economic, environmental, and social impacts.
*   **Business Responsibility Report (BRR) & BRSR:**
    *   Mandated by SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India).
    *   Transitioned to **Business Responsibility and Sustainability Report (BRSR)**, which is mandatory for the top 1000 listed entities by market capitalization. It aligns Indian corporate reporting with global ESG benchmarks.
*   **Integrating SDGs:** Mapping corporate CSR achievements to the UN’s 17 SDGs (e.g., reporting clean-water projects under SDG 6).

#### 3. CSR Audit: Scope, Need, and Procedure
*   **Need & Scope:** Verification of CSR expenditures, evaluating the social impact of projects, and checking compliance with Section 135 rules. It prevents greenwashing and misallocation of funds.
*   **Audit Procedure (PDCA Cycle):**
```
  [Plan] ──► Formulate audit scope, select indicators, and appoint audit personnel.
    ▲                                                                  │
    │                                                                  ▼
  [Act]  ◄── Recommend corrective measures & implement changes ◄── [Check] (Assess outcomes)
```
*   *Plan:* Define objectives, choose audit criteria, and select audit personnel (experts in social work/finance).
*   *Do:* Collect data, interview local communities, and verify financial records of CSR spending.
*   *Check:* Analyze the gap between planned targets and actual social outcomes.
*   *Act:* Report findings to the Board and implement corrective measures to improve future project execution.

### UNIT 13: ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES OF CSR DEPARTMENT

#### 1. CSR Committee vs. CSR Department
*   **CSR Committee:** A board-level committee (mandated under Section 135) that formulates the CSR policy, allocates budgets, and monitors overall compliance.
*   **CSR Department:** The operational unit of the company responsible for executing the approved projects on the ground, coordinate with NGOs, and drafting the annual CSR reports.

#### 2. Tax Implications of CSR (Income Tax Act, 1961)
*   **Section 37(1) Disallowance:** The Finance Act, 2014, amended Section 37(1) to clarify that CSR expenditure is **not allowed as a business expense**. The government reasons that CSR is an application of profit, not a cost incurred for generating business revenue.
*   **Section 80G Deductions:** Companies can claim tax benefits only if they donate to specific government funds listed under Schedule VII (e.g., Clean Ganga Fund, Swachh Bharat Kosh, PM National Relief Fund) which qualify for 100% or 50% deductions under Section 80G.

#### 3. The "Make vs. Buy" Strategic Decision
*   **"Make" (Direct Implementation):** The company executes the CSR projects itself through its own CSR department or corporate foundation (e.g., Infosys Foundation).
    *   *Pros:* High control over quality, direct brand recognition, and close monitoring.
    *   *Cons:* Requires high operational expertise, staff overheads, and local infrastructure.
*   **"Buy" (Partnering/Outsourcing):** The company partners with registered NGOs, trusts, or specialized social enterprises to implement the projects.
    *   *Pros:* Leverages local expertise of NGOs, saves administrative overheads, and speeds up execution.
    *   *Cons:* Low control over daily operations, risk of fund diversion, and diluted brand visibility.

### UNIT 14: CSR AND SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT

#### 1. Concept of Sustainable Development
*   **Definition:** *"Meeting the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs"* (Brundtland Commission Report, 1987).
*   **Key Challenges:** Climate change, greenhouse gas emissions, resource depletion, biodiversity loss, extreme poverty, and social inequality.

#### 2. The Triple Bottom Line (TBL) Approach (John Elkington)
*   TBL argues that corporate success should be measured in three dimensions:
    1.  **Profit (Economic Bottom Line):** Traditional measure of corporate financial health.
    2.  **People (Social Bottom Line):** How the company treats its employees, local community, and general society.
    3.  **Planet (Environmental Bottom Line):** The ecological footprint of the company, focusing on minimizing pollution, waste, and carbon emissions.
```
         [Planet: Environmental]
             ╱             ╲
            ╱               ╲
  [People: Social] ─────── [Profit: Economic]
            \               /
             \   TBL CSR   /
              \           /
               ▼         ▼
        [Sustainable Development]
```

#### 3. Role of Ethics in Sustainability & Corporate Deviance
*   **Ethical Role:** Sustainability is inherently a moral obligation to future generations.
*   **Unethical Practices (Corporate Deviance):**
    *   *Greenwashing:* Falsely projecting a green, eco-friendly image while continuing harmful practices.
    *   *Unethical Lobbying:* Funding campaigns to block environmental regulations.
    *   *Volkswagen Dieselgate:* Installing defeat devices to cheat emission tests to maximize sales while polluting.

#### 4. CSR as an Alternative to Government Rules
*   **Alternative/Complementary View:** Instead of the government setting rigid, top-down regulations, companies voluntarily adopt CSR and sustainability standards.
*   **How Government Encourages CSR:**
    *   *Co-regulation:* Providing legal frameworks like Section 135 to steer corporate funds toward public goals.
    *   *Market Mechanisms:* Setting up the **Social Stock Exchange** to allow social enterprises/corporates to raise funds for developmental goals.
    *   *Recognition:* National CSR Awards to incentivize and showcase best corporate practices.
