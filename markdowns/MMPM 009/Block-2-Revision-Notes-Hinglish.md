## Unit 4: Strategic Retail Planning Process

### 1. Strategic Planning: Definition and Four Approaches
* **Definition**: Strategic planning ek aisa process hai jiske dwara ek retail organization apne future ko envision (kalpana) karti hai aur apne long-term objectives ko achieve karne ke liye zaroori operational procedures, structures, aur systems ko develop karti hai.
* **The Four Strategic Planning Approaches**:
  * **Reactive (Past-oriented)**: Change ko resist karne aur purane "good old days" mein wapas lautne ki koshish. Yeh future ke darr aur current shifts ko undo karne ki chah se chalta hai.
  * **Inactive (Present-oriented)**: Status quo (jaise chal raha hai waise hi chalne dena) ko preserve karne ki ek passive koshish. Organization manti hai ki current state acceptable hai aur future change sirf cheezon ko bigadega, isliye koi action nahi liya jata.
  * **Pre-active (Envisioning the future)**: Future trends (mainly technological aur demographic) ko envision karna aur unka fayda uthane ke liye organization ko in advance position karne ki planning karna.
  * **Pro-active (Creating the future)**: Ek desired future state ko design karna aur use shape dene ke liye actively measures lena. Sirf trends ko anticipate karne ke bajaye, pro-active planners outcomes ko control aur create karte hain.

### 2. Strategy Formulation: Deliberate vs. Emergent
* **Deliberate Strategy**: Structured aur planned hoti hai. Yeh SWOT analysis (internal strengths/weaknesses ko external opportunities/threats se match karna) se shuru hoti hai, competitive aur functional strategies formulate karti hai, aur unhe execute karne ke liye control systems set up karti hai.
* **Emergent Strategy**: Organic aur incremental (dhire-dhire badhne wali) hoti hai. Yeh daily decisions aur problem-solving actions ke consistent series ke through samay ke sath develop hoti hai jo organization ko ek strategic direction mein push karti hai, aur aksar internal organizational politics se influenced hoti hai.

### 3. Corporate and Business Generic Strategies
* **Corporate Level Strategies (Portfolio Scope)**:
  * **Forward Integration**: Distribution channels ya retail outlets par control/ownership gain karna (jaise kisi manufacturer ka apne stores kholna).
  * **Backward Integration**: Supply sources par control/ownership gain karna (jaise apparel retailers ka manufacturing mills ko own karna).
  * **Horizontal Integration**: Market share ko consolidate karne ke liye competitors ko acquire karna ya unke sath merge karna.
  * **Diversification**:
    * *Concentric*: Naye aur related product lines ya businesses ko add karna.
    * *Conglomerate*: Bilkul unrelated product lines ya businesses ko add karna.
  * **Market Penetration**: Existing markets mein existing products ke liye market share ko grow karna.
  * **Market Development**: Existing products ko naye geographical areas ya customer segments mein le jana.
  * **Product Development**: Sales badhane ke liye existing products ko improve karna.
* **Business Level Strategies (Basis of Competition - Porter’s Generic)**:
  * **Cost Leadership**: Cost reductions, cheap fixtures, low rentals, aur high inventory turnover ke through price par compete karna (jaise Walmart, McDonald's).
  * **Differentiation**: Unique features, high customer service, flexibility, aur brand value par compete karna taaki premium prices charge ki ja sakein (jaise Apple Store).
  * **Focus/Niche**: Ek highly specific, narrow market segment ko target karna specialized cost-efficiency ya unique offerings ke sath.

### 4. Performance Audit and Frameworks
* **Performance Audit**: Ek retail business ki health ko analyze karne ka process, jismein dono **"hard" systems** (strategy, operations, IP) aur **"soft" systems** (culture, values, rewards) ko assess kiya jata hai taaki check kiya ja sake ki plan sahi raste par (on course) hai ya nahi.
* **Key Frameworks**:
  * **Gap Analysis**: Actual performance ko desired performance se compare karta hai, "gaps" ko identify karta hai aur resources ko bridge karne ke liye prioritize karta hai.
  * **Balanced Scorecard**: Strategy ko **four balanced drivers** (financial, customer orientation, internal business processes, aur learning & growth) ke cross action mein translate karta hai. Yeh historical financial reports ke sath-sath leading indicators par emphasize karta hai.
  * **Value-Chain Analysis**: Key steps (understanding customer, managing the offering, channel management, location, operations, aur exit strategy) ko dekh kar cause-and-effect linkages ko trace karta hai jo performance ko drive karte hain.

## Unit 5: Models of Retailing

### 1. The Wheel of Retailing Theory
* **Concept**: McNair (1931) dwara propose kiya gaya, yeh batata hai ki retail institutions ek continuous evolutionary cycle se guzarte hain jahan naye formats low-cost/low-price discounters ke roop mein enter karte hain aur dhire-dhire higher-cost/premium operations mein trade up karte hain, jisse wo naye low-end entrants ke liye vulnerable ho jaate hain.
* **The Three Phases**:
  1. **Entry Phase**: Retailer low status, low rentals, low prices, minimal service, aur low profit margin requirements ke sath market mein enter karta hai (jo price-sensitive, non-loyal shoppers ko attract karta hai).
  2. **Trade-Up Phase**: Jaise-jaise retailer success pata hai, wo store locations ko upgrade karke, atmospherics ko enhance karke, services (credit, delivery) add karke, aur prices ko increase karke apne target market ko expand karne ki koshish karta hai.
  3. **Vulnerability Phase**: Retailer high costs, rigid operations, aur premium prices se characterized ho jata hai. Yeh use low-price innovators ki ek nayi wave ke liye vulnerable bana deta hai, aur wheel fir se start ho jata hai.
* *Example*: Walmart aur McDonald's basic low-end concepts ki tarah shuru hue aur dhire-dhire menus/atmospherics ko expand kiya. India mein, Big Bazaar ek discount bargain store ke roop mein enter hua tha.

### 2. Dialectic Process Theory ("Melting Pot" Theory)
* **Concept**: Yeh propose karta hai ki naye retail formats do opposing formats ke clash aur fusion se nikalte hain.
  * **Thesis** (Traditional format) + **Antithesis** (Opposite format) = **Synthesis** (Naya hybrid format).
* *Example*: Traditional physical stores (Thesis) + Direct home-based mail marketing (Antithesis) converge hokar **Electronic Direct Marketing / E-commerce** (Synthesis) banate hain.
* *Example*: Physical retail ka touch-and-feel (Thesis) + Online retail ki borderless reach (Antithesis) fuse hokar **Brick-and-Click / Omnichannel Model** (Synthesis) banate hain.

### 3. Retail Life Cycle Stages
* **Introduction (Early Growth)**: Format bilkul naya hota hai. Low sales, high risk, high launch costs, aur negative/low profits hote hain. Success uncertain hoti hai (jaise early e-commerce entry).
* **Growth (Rapid Development)**: Sales aur profits tezi se expand hote hain. Geographical expansion hota hai. Competitors format ko copy karte hain, aur phase ke end tak cost pressures badhne lagte hain.
* **Maturity**: Market saturation ke karan sales growth slow ho jati hai. High competition ke karan volume maintain karne ke liye margin cuts karne padte hain. Strategic goal decline ko delay karna hota hai.
* **Decline**: Girti hui sales, prices, aur profitability. Retailers ko reposition karna padta hai, format ko abandon karna padta hai, ya niche loyalists ko target karna padta hai (jaise traditional bookstore formats ka e-readers ke aage market khona).

### 4. Theory of Natural Selection (Dreesman)
* **Concept**: Yeh Darwinian evolution ko retail par adapt karta hai. Retail formats vacuum mein evolve nahi hote; unhe survive karne ke liye environmental changes (demographic, economic, technological) ke according continuously adapt karna padta hai. Jo adapt karne mein fail hote hain wo extinct (khatam) ho jaate hain (jaise category killers ke karan specialty toy stores ka fail hona, online presence ke bina traditional bookstores ka decline hona).

### 5. Scrambled Merchandising
* **Definition**: Ek aisi strategy jahan retailer un product lines ya services ko add karta hai jo uski core competencies se completely unrelated hote hain (jaise kisi shoe store ka umbrellas, sweaters, aur handbags bechna; Starbucks ka sandwiches aur salads bechna).
* **Drivers**: Average transaction value badhana, impulse buys ko capture karna, "one-stop shop" convenience provide karna, aur seasonal sales dips ko balance karna.
* **Risks**: Unrelated areas mein retail buying/selling expertise ki kami, high inventory costs, aur agar lines fail ho jayein to brand reputation dilute hone ka risk.

## Unit 6: Based on Ownership

### 1. Independent, Single-Store Ownership Structure
* **Features**: Kisi ek individual ya family dwara own aur run kiya jata hai (jaise Kirana stores, mom-and-pop shops). Centralized, low-layer management hota hai.
* **Advantages**: High operational flexibility, low startup capital, kam licensing hurdles, entrepreneurial zeal, aur local customers ke sath strong personal ties.
* **Limitations**: Low volume purchasing ke karan suppliers ke sath weak bargaining power; manual operations (IT adoption limited hota hai); high failure rate; owner par overdependence, jiske karan strategic planning ki kami hoti hai.

### 2. Corporate Chain Stores
* **Features**: Multiple outlets jo commonly owned hote hain, centrally managed hote hain, aur layout, merchandise & operations ke terms mein standardized hote hain (jaise Trent Westside, Shoppers Stop, Reliance Fresh).
* **Advantages**: Bulk purchasing ke through high bargaining power; marketing/warehousing mein economies of scale; TV/mass media ka access; advanced technology (ERP, VMI) afford karne ki ability.
* **Limitations**: High capital investment (leases, inventory); local micro-markets ke according adapt karne mein rigidity; geographies ke cross administrative complexity; branch employees ke paas limited autonomy.

### 3. Franchising Structures
* **Definition**: Ek contractual agreement jahan ek brand owner (**franchisor**) apne brand, products, aur operating systems ko ek investor (**franchisee**) ko license deta hai in exchange for upfront fees aur ongoing royalties.
* **Forms of Franchising**:
  * **COCO**: Company Owned & Company Operated. Brand dwara full capital aur operations check karna.
  * **FOCO**: Franchise Owned Company Operated. Franchisee setup fund karta hai, brand operations run karta hai.
  * **FICO**: Franchise Invested & Company Operated. Pure investor funding hoti hai, operations brand dwara chalaye jaate hain.
  * **COFO**: Company Owned & Franchisee Operated. Brand own/invest karta hai, franchisee manage karta hai. (Rare).
  * **FOFO**: Franchise Owned & Franchisee Operated. Franchisee invest, own, aur manage karta hai under brand guidelines, and royalties pay karta hai. (Most common).
* **Advantages**:
  * *Franchisee*: Established brand ka access, chota learning curve, training, aur marketing support.
  * *Franchisor*: Rapid geographical expansion with minimal capital outlay; motivated owner-operators.
* **Limitations**:
  * *Franchisee*: Contractual rigidity, gross sales par royalty fees, aur store oversaturation ka risk.
  * *Franchisor*: Agar franchisee quality standards ko violate kare to brand reputation ka risk; high coordination costs.

### 4. Leased Stores / Departments ("Store within a Store")
* **Concept**: Renting out specific departments inside a host store or mall to independent operators (e.g., cosmetics counters in Shoppers Stop, food courts in malls).
* **Advantages**:
  * *Host*: Customers ke liye one-stop shopping, shared overheads, steady rental income, aur experts dwara complex categories ka management.
  * *Lessee*: Host store ke foot traffic ka access, instant credibility, aur lower security/overhead expenses.
* **Limitations**: Dono ke operational hours mismatch ho sakte hain, aur lessee ki mistakes ke liye customers host store ko blame karte hain.

### 5. Consumer Cooperatives
* **Concept**: Middlemen ko eliminate karne aur prices ko low rakhne ke liye cooperative societies ya consumer groups dwara owned aur run kiye jaane wale retail shops (jaise Kendriya Bhandar, Sahakar Bhandar).
* **Advantages**: Lower prices, reliable quality, aur measurement fraud (maap-tol mein gadbadi) se bachav.
* **Limitations**: Limited marketing/publicity; skilled management ki kami; members ka disinterest.

### 6. Vertical Marketing System (VMS)
* **Concept**: Conventional channels ke opposite jahan manufacturers, wholesalers, aur retailers independently operate karte hain, ek VMS efficiency ko maximize karne ke liye channel members ko coordinate ya integrate karta hai.
  * **Independent VMS**: Separate entities loosely coordinate karte hain. Stationery aur grocery trades ke liye standard hai.
  * **Partially Integrated VMS**: Do levels ko ek hi member own karta hai (jaise manufacturer-owned retail outlets).
  * **Fully Integrated VMS**: Sourcing, manufacturing, wholesaling, aur retailing ek hi corporate umbrella ke under control hote hain (jaise HUL, ITC, Reliance).

### 7. Dealership and Network Marketing
* **Dealership**: Ek licensing model jahan dealer ko manufacturer ke products sell karne ke rights milte hain. Dealer ek independent retailer ki tarah behave karta hai, use guaranteed supply/support milti hai, aur wo koi royalty pay nahi karta (jaise Maruti Suzuki ya GM car dealers).
* **Network Marketing**: Ek direct-selling model jahan independent agents apne personal network (family, friends) ko products sell karke aur sub-agents ko recruit karke commission earn karte hain (jaise Amway, Tupperware).

## Unit 7: Store and Non-Store Based Retail Formats

### 1. Classification of Store Formats
* **Specialty Store**: Narrow product line, deep assortment. Yeh expert staff aur customer service par emphasize karta hai (jaise Zara, Titan EyePlus).
* **Department Store**: Product lines ki broad variety (apparel, home, furniture) jo specialist buyers dwara manage kiye jaane wale alag-alag departments mein organized hoti hai (jaise Shoppers Stop, Lifestyle).
* **Supermarket**: Low margins aur high volume ke sath self-service food aur grocery store (~4K-5K sq. ft., jaise Reliance Fresh).
* **Convenience Store**: Extended hours ke sath choti neighborhood shop, jo high-turnover daily necessities ko higher prices par bechti hai (jaise 7-Eleven, Spencer’s Daily).
* **Superstore/Hypermarket**: Supermarkets aur discount stores ko combine karne wale mass merchandisers (~50K-100K sq. ft.). High volume, low margins, aur check-out lanes hote hain (jaise DMart, Spencer's Hyper).
* **Off-Price Retailer**: Deep discounts par surplus, irregular, ya overstocked branded items ko sell karta hai. Isme shamil hain:
  * **Factory Outlets**: Manufacturer-owned stores jo clearance/excess stock sell karte hain (jaise Nike Factory Outlet).
  * **Warehouse Clubs**: Member-only discount warehouses (jaise Costco, Sam's Club).
* **Catalogue Showroom**: Warehouse ke paas bana retail showroom. Customers catalogs/displays browse karte hain, items select karte hain, aur unhe directly warehouse hatch se receive karte hain (jaise Urban Ladder, FabIndia).

### 2. Classification of Non-Store Formats
* **Direct Marketing**: Phone, mail, TV, ya social media ke through customers tak pahunchkar ek direct order response trigger karna (bina face-to-face contact ke).
* **Direct Selling**: Consumers ke homes ya offices mein face-to-face personal selling karna (jaise door-to-door sales, party plans).
* **Automatic Vending**: Impersonal vending machines jo high-turnover, low-cost convenience goods (soft drinks, snacks) ko 24/7 sell karti hain. High equipment costs ke karan retail prices thode badh jaate hain.
* **E-tailing (Electronic Retailing)**: Online products sell karna. Yeh segmented hai **B2C** (retail to consumer, jaise Amazon, Nykaa) aur **B2B** (businesses ke beech wholesale trade, jaise software licensing, raw material supply) mein.
