### UNIT 11: ETHICS IN RESEARCH

#### 1. Concept of Research Ethics
Research ethics un moral benchmarks, standards, aur rules ko kehte hain jo researcher ke behaviour aur decisions ko guide karte hain. Iska basic target yeh ensure karna hota hai ki **research se kisi bhi respondent, stakeholder ya society ko koi nuksan, dhokha, ya negative impact na jhelna pade**.

#### 2. Twelve Core Principles of Research Ethics (Research Ethics ke 12 Rules)
1.  **Honesty:** Data collection, results, methods, aur publication details me bilkul sachai (truthfulness) bartein. Galtiyan ya fake data mat show karein.
2.  **Integrity:** Jo waada kiya hai use nibhaayein, research agreements par date points follow karein. Unrealistic expectations na badhayein.
3.  **Objectivity:** Data analysis, experimental design, aur research evaluation me personal biases aur subjective views ko active minimize karein.
4.  **Informed Consent:** Har participant study ke consequences, profits aur risk profiles ko achi tarah samajhkar voluntary (apni marzi se) research join kare.
5.  **Beneficence:** Respondents aur society ko maximum benefits dena aur participants ke potential threats aur risk limits ko minimum set rakhna.
6.  **Protecting Subjects:** Respondents ki human dignity, personal rights, aur individual choices ka respect karna.
7.  **Confidentiality:** Participants ke identity, records, aur responses ko clean safety zones me lock rakhna taaki leaks na hon.
8.  **Responsible Publication:** Duplicate submissions ya bar-bar same research content publish karne se bachein (self-plagiarism avoid karein).
9.  **Non-Discrimination:** Kisi bhi individual se gender, religion, race, age, ya caste ke basis par partiality na karein.
10. **Openness:** Constructive criticism, naye ideas aur feed-backs ko positively handle karein.
11. **Carefulness:** Research details ko double-check karein taaki silly mistakes aur negligence failures na hon.
12. **Respect for Intellectual Property:** Dusro ke patents, copyrights, aur research writings ko respect karein. Bina proper reference credit ke kisi ka intellectual work use na karein.

#### 3. Three Major Research Misconducts (Research ki 3 Sabse Badi Galtiyan)
*   **Fabrication:** Bilkul fake experiments aur raw data generate karke unhe real survey report batana.
*   **Falsification:** Experiment procedures ya data metrics me thod-marod (manipulation/modification) karna taaki desired output jabardasti prove ho sake.
*   **Plagiarism:** Kisi doosre insaan ki research lines, concepts, images ya results ko bina credit diye chura kar apna batana.
    *   *How to Avoid Plagiarism:* Dusre papers ka reference lete waqt unhe achi tarah **paraphrase** (apne words me likhna) karein, exact matched sentences par quotation marks lagayein, in-text citations add karein, aur software check standard ensure karein.

### UNIT 12: SUBSTANCE OF REPORTS

#### 1. Academic vs. Business Reports
*   **Academic Reports:** Inka primary focus deep conceptual study, theoretical understanding aur literature body ko enrich karne me hota hai (term papers, projects, theses, dissertations).
*   **Business (Managerial) Reports:** Inka core aim executive decision-making ko support karna hota hai. Yeh reports highly action-oriented, short, aur business issues ko immediately solve karne wali hoti hain.

#### 2. The Research Proposal
Proposal study start karne se pehle ek detailed written design/budget draft hota hai jo researcher aur funding sponsor ke agreement base ko clarify karta hai.
*   **Ten Key Components of a Proposal (Proposal ke 10 Essential Parts):**
    1. Descriptive Title of the Study.
    2. Authors details aur research background profile.
    3. Nature of study (Problem outline, relevance, and target reader group).
    4. Working hypotheses jo testing me work aayengi.
    5. Data variables plans (Sources, dynamic methods, processing tools).
    6. Special software ya lab facilities requirement profile.
    7. Detailed project schedule aur target deadlines (Milestone milestones charts).
    8. Tentative chapter outline layout of the final report.
    9. Detailed cost budgets.
    10. Dynamic reference bibliography draft.

#### 3. Three Categories of Reports
1.  **Information-Oriented Reports:** Kisi topic/event ka complete descriptive image frame display karna. Yeh check karne ke liye **6 Ws + 1 H** framework use hota hai: *Who does What to Whom, When, Where, How, and Why?*
2.  **Decision-Oriented Reports:** Problem-solving system framework follow karta hai. Main steps:
    *   *Problem Outline:* Symptom vs Core cause separation, Kepner-Tregoe matrix analysis (sorting variables into *Is / Is Not* columns).
    *   *Criteria Construction:* SWOT analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) apply karke feasibility benchmarks set karna.
    *   *Option Generation:* Creative choices develop karna, screening matrix banana, aur criteria scale par check karna.
    *   *Action & Contingency Plans:* Kaun kya aur kab karega implement plan banana, aur plan collapse hone ke situation me emergency bailing plans ready rakhna.
3.  **Research-Oriented Reports:** Subject knowledge limits ko pull karne ke liye detailed sequential setup: Literature survey $\rightarrow$ Hypotheses $\rightarrow$ Methodology $\rightarrow$ Data description $\rightarrow$ Findings $\rightarrow$ Conclusions $\rightarrow$ Recommendations.

#### 4. Six Areas for Reviewing a Draft (Draft check karne ke 6 points)
Final print se pehle draft me in parameters ko closely monitor karein:
1.  **Author's Purpose:** Kya report ka real goal explicit aur logical lag raha hai?
2.  **Reader's Profile:** Kya writing tone aur language readers' hierarchy (Boss vs colleague), educational background, aur dynamic needs se properly aligned hai?
3.  **Content:** Kya start se hi focus clear hai? Facts verified hain aur calculations accurate hain?
4.  **Language and Tone:** Kya sentence frame simplified aur technical jargon overflow se completely free hai?
5.  **Length:** Kya data concise hai ya unnecessary lines se reader ka time waste ho raha hai?
6.  **Appearance:** Formatting, spacing patterns, binding layout kitna premium aur professional lag raha hai.

### UNIT 13: FORMATS OF REPORTS

#### 1. Parts of a Report (Report ke Dynamic Components)
Ek standard complete report in segments me divided hoti hai:
*   **Cover and Title Page:** Title name, date, target audience, and safety/security limits label (e.g., *"For internal circulation only"*).
*   **Introductory Pages:**
    *   *Foreword & Preface:* Research context aur introductory details.
    *   *Acknowledgements:* Research partners aur supporters ko gratitude show karna.
    *   *Table of Contents:* Dynamic index of chapters aur dynamic page index.
    *   *List of Tables/Illustrations:* Data charts index directory.
    *   *Executive Summary:* Poore report ka high-impact standalone summary, jisme variables, core results aur major recommendations display hon.
*   **Text (Main Body):** actual chapters with headers, footnotes, tables aur visual diagrams.
*   **Reference Section:**
    *   *Appendices:* Large raw sheets, dynamic formulas, ya questionnaire copies display area.
    *   *Bibliography:* dynamic list of dynamic resources.
    *   *Glossary:* Dynamic definitions of complex technical terms.

#### 2. Copyreading vs. Proofreading
*   **Copyreading:** Manuscript ko printer ke paas bhejne se pehle check karna. Focus is on grammar correction, tone selection, styling patterns, and fact checks.
*   **Proofreading:** Typesetting (composing room) ke baad printed sheet paper ko check karna. Focus is strictly on identifying typographical (typing) mistakes aur layout slips that printer introduced.

#### 3. How to Proofread (Hinglish Rules)
*   **Letter-by-Letter:** Words padhne ke bajaye letter-by-letter check karein taaki small spelling errors easily trap ho sakein.
*   **Margin Marks:** Corrections hamesha **line margin zone** me standard symbols ke sath likhein. Printer center lines check nahi karta, woh sirf left/right empty margin check karega.
*   **Stages of Proofs:**
    1.  *Galley Proofs (First Proofs):* Lambe continuous rolls sheets jo page template sections me break na hon (up to 24 inches long).
    2.  *Page Proofs:* Actual page layout templates matching index page setup.
    3.  *Press Dummy:* Final actual mock version of book before dynamic dynamic print operations.
*   **No Editing:** Proof stage standard correct parameters ke liye hai, naya re-writing edit operations yahan completely avoid karein (Author Alterations [AA] at this stage are highly slow and expensive).

### UNIT 14: PRESENTATION OF A REPORT

#### 1. Core Presentation Skills
Ek superb dynamic analytical report bhi waste ho sakti hai agar delivery bad hai. Success strictly client requirements ko samajhne, baseline message clear rakhne, visual transitions perfect rakhne, aur eye contact handle karne par depend karti hai.

#### 2. Classification of Audio-Visual (AV) Aids
```
                           AUDIO-VISUAL AIDS
                                   │
         ┌─────────────────────────┼─────────────────────────┐
         ▼                         ▼                         ▼
       AUDIO                    VISUAL                 AUDIO-VISUAL
  (Tape Recorder, CD)              │                 (Video, Film, TV)
                 ┌─────────────────┴─────────────────┐
                 ▼                                   ▼
           NON-PROJECTED                         PROJECTED
      (Blackboard, Flip Chart)            (Overhead, Slides, Epidiascope)
```

#### 3. Crucial Advantages of AV Aids
*   **Credibility & Clarity:** Complex concepts ko visual mini shape me dikhata hai, jisse clarity aur validation high level secure hota hai.
*   **Retention & Recall:** sound and sight sensory channels ek sath use karne se listeners ka recall rate aur target attention boost ho jata hai.
*   **Collapsing Distance & Time:** Alag region ke dynamic parameters aur visual patterns dynamically classroom me direct project kiye ja sakte hain.

#### 4. Essential Precautions for Presenting with AV Aids (AV use karne ki precautions)
*   **Screen-Staring Avoid Karein:** Kabhi bhi audience ki taraf back karke slide reading start na karein. slides listeners ke reference ke liye hain, aapke reading script ke liye nahi.
*   **The 6x6 Rule:** Slides me clutter avoid karein. Max 6 bullet points per slide, aur max 6 words per bullet block rule follow karein.
*   **Legibility Check:** font spacing, contrast ratio aur visual size check karein taaki last bench student ko bhi clear read ho.
*   **Technical Rehearsal:** Seminar hall me 15 mins pehle pohoch kar dynamic projector compatibility, light scale aur sound metrics check karein. Power loss control ke liye emergency backup plans (e.g., printed papers) ready rakhein.
*   **Cue Cards:** Heavy books or directories carry karne ke bajaye brief cues pointers cards use karein taaki delivery natural rahe.
