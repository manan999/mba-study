### UNIT 12: CSR REPORTING PROCESS & AUDITING

#### 1. Concept and Rationale of CSR Reporting (CSR Reporting kya hai aur iski kya zaroorat hai)
*   **Definition:** Ek aisa process jisme organization stakeholders ko apni social, environmental, aur ethical performance ke baare me report karti hai.
*   **Rationale (Zaroorat):**
    1.  *Transparency (Pardarshita):* Customers, investors, aur community me company ke prati trust badhta hai.
    2.  *Legitimacy (Kanooni manyata):* Kanoon aur rules (jaise SEBI regulations) ke compliance ko show karta hai.
    3.  *Internal Improvement:* Company ko apne operations ki inefficiencies aur material waste ko identify karne me help milti hai.

#### 2. Tools and Techniques of CSR Reporting (CSR Reporting ke tarike)
*   **GRI (Global Reporting Initiative):** Puri duniya me sabse zyada use hone wala standard sustainability reporting ke liye, jo economic, social, aur environmental impact par focus karta hai.
*   **Business Responsibility Report (BRR) & BRSR:**
    *   SEBI dwara mandate kiya gaya report format.
    *   Ab iska modern form **Business Responsibility and Sustainability Report (BRSR)** hai, jo top 1000 listed entities ke liye mandatory hai. Yeh global ESG requirements ke sath align hai.
*   **Integrating SDGs:** CSR projects ko UN ke 17 SDGs (Sustainable Development Goals) ke sath map karna (e.g., clean water projects ko SDG 6 ke under show karna).

#### 3. CSR Audit: Scope, Need, and Procedure (CSR Audit ki zaroorat aur procedure)
*   **Need & Scope:** CSR spending ko verify karna, actual social impact ko assess karna, aur Section 135 ke rules ko compliance check karna. Isse greenwashing aur budget misuse rukta hai.
*   **Audit Procedure (PDCA Cycle):**
```
  [Plan] ──► Audit scope, indicators set karna, aur audit team select karna.
    ▲                                                                 │
    │                                                                 ▼
  [Act]  ◄── Corrective recommendations aur changes implement karna ◄── [Check] (Impact check)
```
*   *Plan (Yojana):* Audit objectives choose karna aur appropriate team members (experts/chartered accountants) select karna.
*   *Do (Karna):* Fields se data collect karna, local communities se interviews lena, aur financial files check karna.
*   *Check (Jaanch):* Planned social targets aur actual impact ke beech ka gap analyze karna.
*   *Act (Karya):* Audit report board ko submit karna aur corrections apply karna.

### UNIT 13: ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES OF CSR DEPARTMENT

#### 1. CSR Committee vs. CSR Department (Dono me kya antar hai)
*   **CSR Committee:** Board-level committee hoti hai (Under Section 135) jo policy banati hai, budgets pass karti hai, aur compliance monitor karti hai.
*   **CSR Department:** Operational unit hoti hai jo actually projects ko ground level par execute karti hai, NGOs se tie-up karti hai, aur audit documentation ready karti hai.

#### 2. Tax Implications of CSR (CSR par tax rules - Income Tax Act, 1961)
*   **Section 37(1) Disallowance:** Finance Act, 2014, ke mutabik CSR expenditure ko **business expense (vyavasayik kharcha) nahi mana jata**. Government ka manna hai ki CSR profit kamane ka tool nahi hai, balki profit hone ke baad ka application hai. Isliye is par regular business tax deduction nahi milta.
*   **Section 80G Deductions:** Deductions sirf tabhi milti hain jab company Schedule VII ke specific government funds me daan deti hai (e.g., PM National Relief Fund, Clean Ganga Fund, Swachh Bharat Kosh) jo 80G ke under exempt hain.

#### 3. The "Make vs. Buy" Strategic Decision (Direct execution vs. Outsourcing)
*   **"Make" (Direct Implementation):** Company apne CSR department ya corporate foundation (e.g., Infosys Foundation) ke through direct projects karti hai.
    *   *Pros (Fayde):* Execution par full control rehta hai, direct brand visibility milti hai, aur quality ensure hoti hai.
    *   *Cons (Nuksan):* Zyaada manpower lagti hai, management overheads aate hain, aur direct local connectivity build karni padti hai.
*   **"Buy" (NGO Partnership):** Company social organizations ya registered NGOs ko project outsource karti hai.
    *   *Pros (Fayde):* NGOs ke paas local experience aur connectivity hoti hai, administration costs bach jaate hain, aur fast delivery hoti hai.
    *   *Cons (Nuksan):* Operations par direct control nahi rehta, funding redirection/misuse ka risk hota hai, aur direct brand recognition nahi mil pati.

### UNIT 14: CSR AND SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT

#### 1. Concept of Sustainable Development (Satat Vikas kya hai)
*   **Definition:** *"Future generations ki needs se samjhauta kiye bina, present generation ki needs ko poora karna"* (Brundtland Commission Report, 1987).
*   **Key Challenges:** Climate change, greenhouse gas emission, ecological damage, deforestation, resource limits, aur social inequality.

#### 2. The Triple Bottom Line (TBL) Approach (John Elkington का TBL model)
*   Corporate success ko sirf profit se nahi, balki teen dimensions se check kiya jana chahiye:
    1.  **Profit (Economic):** Company ki financial health.
    2.  **People (Social):** Employees, community, aur society ka welfare.
    3.  **Planet (Environmental):** Eco-friendliness, waste management, aur carbon emission control.

  [People: Samaj] ──────── [Profit: Aarthik]

#### 3. Role of Ethics in Sustainability & Corporate Deviance (Naitikta aur Unethical Practices)
*   **Ethical Role:** Sustainability aane wali peedhi (future generations) ke prati humari moral responsibility hai.
*   **Corporate Deviance (Unethical practices):**
    *   *Greenwashing:* Jhootha dhawa karna ki company environment-friendly hai, jabki actual me pollution kar rahi ho.
    *   *Unethical Lobbying:* Eco-friendly laws aur regulations ko block karne ke liye governments ko target/fund karna.
    *   *Volkswagen Dieselgate:* Cars me test-cheating software lagakar air quality regulations ko break karna taaki sales aur profit badhaya ja sake.

#### 4. CSR as an Alternative to Government Rules (Kanoon ke badle CSR)
*   **Alternative/Complementary View:** Rigid government guidelines ki jagah corporations voluntarily (apni marzi se) CSR standards adopt karti hain.
*   **How Government Encourages CSR (Sarkar kaise badhawa deti hai):**
    *   *Co-regulation:* Section 135 jaise frameworks banana taaki corporate sector public goals me help kare.
    *   *Market Mechanisms:* **Social Stock Exchange** banakar social business models ko fund raise karne ki facility dena.
    *   *Recognition:* National CSR Awards aur certificates dena.
