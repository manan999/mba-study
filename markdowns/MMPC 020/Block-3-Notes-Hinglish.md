### UNIT 9: CSR IN INDIAN CONTEXT

#### 1. Four Historical Phases of CSR in India (India me CSR ke 4 historical phases)
*   **Phase 1 (1850 - 1914): Merchant Charity & Philanthropy (Vyapari daan aur dharmada):**
    *   Mainly family-owned businesses (Tatas, Birlas, Godrej) aur religious values ke through run hota tha.
    *   Daan-dharm unstructured tha aur iska focus mandir, dharamsala, paani ke talab banwana, aur famine (akaal) ke waqt relief dene par tha.
*   **Phase 2 (1914 - 1960): Trusteeship Model & Freedom Struggle (Gandhi ji ka trusteeship):**
    *   Mahatma Gandhi ke **Trusteeship** model se deeply influenced tha, jisme rich industrialists ko apni wealth ko public welfare ke liye "trustee" ki tarah manage karne ke liye kaha gaya.
    *   CSR national goals ke sath align ho gaya, jaise educational institutions set up karna, rural development, aur independence movement ko financial support dena.
*   **Phase 3 (1960 - 1990): Mixed Economy & State-Led Regulations (License Raj ka samay):**
    *   Is phase me government rules bahut strict ho gaye ("License Raj"). Development ki main responsibility government ki thi aur private business par restrictions the.
    *   CSR peripheral (side activity) ban gaya, jahan companies public sector enterprises (PSUs) aur state welfare schemes me contribute karti thin.
*   **Phase 4 (1990 onwards): Globalization & Strategic Integration (Liberalization ke baad):**
    *   Economic reforms se MNCs India aayi. CSR voluntary charity se aage badhkar strategic business practice me convert ho gaya.
    *   Iska end result **Companies Act, 2013** ke roop me aaya, jisne India ko mandatory CSR spending lagane wala pehla desh banaya.

#### 2. Key CSR Initiatives of Indian Companies (Badi Indian companies ke CSR initiatives)
*   **Tata Group:** CSR ke pioneers (e.g., Tata Steel rural welfare schemes, Tata Memorial Cancer Hospital, aur IISc, TIFR jaise world-class institutes ko initial funding).
*   **ITC Limited:** Apne **e-Choupal** project ke liye famous hai. Rural villages me internet kiosks lagakar farmers ko direct buyers se joda, intermediaries (bichauliyon) ko hataya, aur productivity badhayi.
*   **Infosys Foundation:** Healthcare, education, rural development, art, aur culture ke sector me support provide karta hai.
*   **Wipro (Azim Premji Foundation):** Primary aur secondary school education ki quality aur equality ko improve karne par focused hai.

### UNIT 10: CSR LEGISLATION AND POLICY GUIDELINES

#### 1. Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013 (Applicability aur Rules)
*   **Applicability Criteria (Kin companies par lagta hai):** Har company (chahe private ho, public ho, ya foreign office ho India me) jo kisi financial year me niche diye gaye conditions me se kisi ek ko meet karti ho:
    1.  **Net Worth:** ₹500 crore ya usse zyada.
    2.  **Turnover (Salan bikri):** ₹1000 crore ya usse zyada.
    3.  **Net Profit (Shudh labh):** ₹5 crore ya usse zyada.
*   **Spending Requirement (Kitna spend karna hai):** Pichle 3 financial years ke average net profit ka at least **2% mandatory CSR spend** karna hoga.
*   **CSR Committee:**
    *   Isme 3 ya usse zyada directors hone chahiye, jisme kam se kam 1 independent director ho.
    *   *Exemption (Choot):* Agar CSR obligation **₹50 lakh** se kam hai, toh CSR Committee banana zaroori nahi hai. Board of Directors hi iska responsibility handle kar lenge.
*   **Treatment of Unspent Amount (Agar paisa unspent reh jaye):**
    *   *Ongoing Projects (Chal rahe projects):* Unspent amount ko year-end ke 30 days ke andar ek special bank account ("Unspent CSR Account") me transfer karna hoga aur 3 financial years ke andar spend karna hoga. Agar tab bhi nahi hua, toh Schedule VII fund me transfer karna hoga.
    *   *Other Projects:* Financial year khatam hone ke 6 months ke andar directly Schedule VII fund (e.g., PM National Relief Fund) me daalna hoga.

#### 2. Schedule VII: Permitted CSR Activities (Qualifying Activities)
Schedule VII me un activities ki list hai jo CSR spend ke andar count hoti hain, jaise:
*   Bhookh, poverty, aur malnutrition (kuposhan) dur karna, healthcare aur sanitation (Swachh Bharat) badhana.
*   Education, vocational skills, aur livelihood training ko promote karna.
*   Gender equality, women empowerment, aur anathalayon ya old-age homes ko support dena.
*   Environmental sustainability, ecological balance, aur animal welfare (pashu kalyan) maintain karna.
*   National heritage, art, aur culture ki protection.
*   PM National Relief Fund, Clean Ganga Fund, Swachh Bharat Kosh, etc. me contribution dena.

#### 3. CSR Policy, Monitoring, and Impact Assessment (Niti, Nigrani aur Prabhav Aakalan)
*   **CSR Policy Components:** Outline ki kaun se projects karne hain, implementation schedules, budget allocation, aur monitoring procedures kya honge.
*   **Duties of the Board:** CSR policy approve karna, website par display karna, 2% spending ensure karna, aur unspent paise ka reason Board's Report me explain karna.
*   **Impact Assessment (Prabhav Aakalan):**
    *   Un companies ke liye mandatory hai jinka pichle 3 years me average CSR obligation **₹10 crore ya usse zyada** ho.
    *   Un projects ke liye impact assessment karwana hoga jinka outlay **₹1 crore ya usse zyada** ho, aur project complete hue 1 saal se zyada ho gaya ho (independent agency ke through).
    *   Impact assessment expenses ko CSR spend me add kiya ja sakta hai (maximum 2% of total CSR spend ya ₹50 lakh, whichever is higher).

### UNIT 11: CSR IN PUBLIC SECTOR UNITS (PSUs)

#### 1. Department of Public Enterprises (DPE) Guidelines for CPSEs (Sarkari companies ke guidelines)
*   Central Public Sector Enterprises (CPSEs) ka double mandate hota hai: profit kamana aur social development karna (model employers banna).
*   **Key DPE Guidelines:**
    1.  **Mandatory Budget Allocation:** Section 135 ke mutabik spend karna aur local area ko priority dena jahan unke plants/operations hain.
    2.  **CSR and Sustainability Integration:** CSR projects sustainable development parameters (renewable energy, water harvesting) ke sath aligned hone chahiye.
    3.  **Social Audit:** Projects ki physical completion aur social impact verify karne ke liye social audits regular interval par karwane honge.

#### 2. National Voluntary Guidelines (NVGs), 2011
*   Ministry of Corporate Affairs dwara release kiye gaye **9 Core Principles** jo businesses ko socially responsible banate hain:
    1.  Ethical governance, transparency, aur accountability rakhna.
    2.  Sustainable goods aur services provide karna.
    3.  Employees ke well-being aur safety ko promote karna.
    4.  Vulnerable (kamzor) stakeholders ke interests ke prati responsive rehna.
    5.  Human rights ko respect aur promote karna.
    6.  Environment ko protect aur restore karne ke efforts karna.
    7.  Public policy ko responsibly influence karna.
    8.  Inclusive growth aur barabar development ko support karna.
    9.  Customers ko responsible manner me values dena.

#### 3. Global CSR Guidelines (Vishwavyapi Guidelines)
*   **UN Global Compact (UNGC):** Human rights, labor, environment, aur anti-corruption ke 10 principles.
*   **OECD Guidelines:** Government dwara MNCs ko di gayi recommendations.
*   **ISO 26000:** Social responsibility par guidance dene wala international standard (certification ke liye nahi hai).
*   **GRI (Global Reporting Initiative):** Sustainability reporting ka standard framework.
