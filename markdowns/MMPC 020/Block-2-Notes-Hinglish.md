### UNIT 5: CSR: AN OVERVIEW

#### 1. Concept of Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)
*   **Definition:** Business ka commitment jisse woh sustainable economic development me contribute kare—employees, unke families, local community, aur society ke sath milkar unki quality of life ko improve karne ke liye.
*   **Evolution of Focus:** CSR ab philanthropy (voluntary charity jo business strategy se bilkul alag hoti thi) se badalkar core business strategy aur integrated agenda ban gaya hai.

#### 2. Shareholder Primacy vs. Stakeholder Primacy (Shareholder vs. Stakeholder Prathamta)
*   **Shareholder Primacy (Milton Friedman):** Managers shareholders ke agents hote hain aur unka fiduciary duty sirf profit maximize karna hai. Company ka paisa social causes par lagana bina permission taxation karne jaisa hai.
*   **Stakeholder Primacy (R. Edward Freeman):** Firm ek social institution hai. Stakeholders ka matlab un sabhi groups ya individuals se hai jo company ke decisions se affect hote hain ya company ko affect kar sakte hain.
*   **Classification of Stakeholders (Stakeholders ke types):**
    *   *Primary Stakeholders:* Jo firm ke survival ke liye bohot zaroori hain (e.g., shareholders, employees, customers, suppliers).
    *   *Secondary Stakeholders:* Jo direct sales/survial me involve nahi hote par firm ko affect karte hain (e.g., media, CSOs, trade associations).

#### 3. Stakeholders as Drivers of CSR (CSR ko chalane wale drivers)
*   Stakeholders CSR ke strong drivers ban kar ubhre hain:
    1.  **Consumers:** Aajkal consumers products kharidne se pehle companies ki ethics aur social activities ko check karte hain.
    2.  **Employees:** Employees aisi companies me kaam karna pasand karte hain jo socially responsible hon, isse retention rate badhta hai.
    3.  **Government:** Kanoon ke zariye (jaise India me Section 135) companies ko mandatory spending ke liye force karti hai.
    4.  **Civil Society (CSOs):** Watchdogs ki tarah kaam karti hain jo environmental aur social violations ko highlight karti hain.
    5.  **Investors (SRI/ESG):** Investors unhi companies me capital invest karte hain jo environmental, social, aur governance standards par khari utarti hain.

#### 4. Corporate Citizenship (Corporate Nagrikta)
*   **Concept:** Yeh view karta hai ki corporation bhi society ka ek member (citizen) hai, isliye uske paas rights ke sath-sath duties bhi hain. Companies ko responsibly behave karna chahiye aur public problems ko solve karne me partner banna chahiye.

### UNIT 6: BUSINESS STRATEGY IN CSR

#### 1. Integrating CSR into Management Practices (CSR ko business me shaamil karna)
*   **Peripheral CSR (Bahari CSR):** CSR ko main business se alag rakhna (e.g., charity outsource kar dena). Isse temporary legitimacy toh milti hai par long-term corporate ya social value generate nahi hoti.
*   **Integrated CSR (Aantrik CSR):** CSR ko main business activities, core competencies, aur daily decisions me embed karna.

#### 2. Four Strategic Approaches to CSR (CSR ke 4 Strategic Approaches)
1.  **Cost and Risk Reduction (Kharch aur Risk kam karna):**
    *   *Trade-off Hypothesis (Friedman):* View ki CSR par kharch karne se company ke costs badhte hain aur profitability kam hoti hai.
    *   *Slack Resources Theory:* Companies CSR tabhi karti hain jab unke paas surplus resources (slack capital) available hon aur unhe profit ki chinta na ho.
    *   *Enlightened Value Maximization:* Stakeholder trade-offs ko responsibly manage karna taaki risks (jaise local protests) kam ho sakein aur long-term profit badhe.
2.  **Competitive Advantage (Pratiyogita me aage nikalna):**
    *   *Strategic Philanthropy (Porter & Kramer):* Philanthropy ko aise align karna jisse company ka business prospects improve ho (e.g., Cisco ne IT network administrators train karne ke liye networking Academy banayi).
    *   *Base of the Pyramid (BoP) Approach:* Subse poor segment (BoP) ke liye affordable products banana taaki social problem solve hone ke sath new market create ho.
3.  **Reputation and Legitimacy (Reputation aur Kanooni manyata):**
    *   *Social License to Operate:* Local community ka trust aur approval jeetna. Mining aur paper industries me yeh bahut zaroori hai taaki community protest ke karan operations band na hon.
    *   *Cause-Related Marketing (CRM):* Apne products ki sales ko social cause se link karna (e.g., har purchase par a percentage of sales charity me jana) jisse customers brand se connect hote hain aur sales badhti hai.
4.  **Synergistic Value Creation (Sabke liye value banana):**
    *   *Sustainable Local Enterprise Network (SLEN):* NGOs, local communities, aur businesses ka network jo social aur financial goals ko sath lekar chalta hai (e.g., **Honey Care Africa** aur **Grameen Shakti**).

#### 3. Honey Care Africa & Grameen Shakti Case Studies
*   **Honey Care Africa (Kenya):** Rural farmers ko hives dilwaye (microfinance and NGO help se) aur unse fixed/guaranteed price par honey purchase kiya. Isse rural income badhi, biodiversity maintain hui (incentivized reforestation), aur World Bank investment mila.
*   **Grameen Shakti (Bangladesh):** Ek not-for-profit model jo solar energy panels ko microloans ke through rural areas me provide karta hai. Grameen Bank ke network se connect hone ke karan inhone 40,000+ solar systems install kiye jisse educational aur health levels me sudhaar aaya.

### UNIT 7: CSR IN GLOBAL CONTEXT

#### 1. CSR in Global Supply Chains (Global supply chains me CSR)
*   **The Issue:** MNCs production costs kam karne ke liye developing countries me manufacturing outsource karti hain jahan labor laws weak hote hain.
*   **Ethical Challenges:** Exploitative sweatshops, child labor, aur unsafe working environment (e.g., Rana Plaza accident in Bangladesh).
*   **Supply Chain CSR:** MNCs ko apne suppliers ke liye strict Supplier Code of Conduct banana aur impact checking ke liye audits karna zaroori hai taaki global reputation kharab na ho.

#### 2. Prevalent Forms of Social Activism (Social activism ke dominant forms)
*   **Consumer Activism:** Unethical brands ko boycott karna aur ethical/fair-trade products ko purchase karna.
*   **CSO/NGO Activism:** Campaigns, media reports, aur legal pressure se corporates ko answerable banana.
*   **Shareholder Activism:** Investors dwara AGM/resolutions me boards ko ESG guidelines follow karne ke liye force karna.
*   **Employee Activism:** Employees dwara walkouts ya whistleblowing karna jab company unethical project me involve ho.

#### 3. CSR and Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)
*   UN ke 17 SDGs global challenges (jaise poverty, climate change, gender equality) ko address karne ke liye banaye gaye hain. Companies apne CSR programs ko in SDGs ke sath map karti hain taaki compliance aur brand goodwill ban sake.

### UNIT 8: BUSINESS ETHICS AND CSR: LINKAGES

#### 1. Drawing the Linkages (Dono ke beech ka connection)
*   **Business Ethics:** Conceptual aur philosophical foundation hai (kya sahi hai aur kya galat, uske principles).
*   **CSR:** Business ethics ka practical aur visible expression hai. Yeh woh action hai jo firm apne ethical commitments ko poora karne ke liye leti hai.
```
  [Business Ethics: Siddhant] ───► [CSR: Kriya / Action]
```

#### 2. Corporate Benefits of Ethical Codes and CSR (CSR aur Ethics ke corporate benefits)
*   **Financial Performance:** Corporate Social Performance (CSP) aur Corporate Financial Performance (CFP) ke beech positive correlation hota hai.
*   **Intangible Assets:**
    *   *Reputation:* Trust badhne se customers high price pay karne ko ready hote hain aur brand loyalty badhti hai.
    *   *Talent Attraction:* Employees motivate hote hain, productiveness badhti hai, aur employee turnover/attrition cost kam hota hai.
    *   *Capital Access:* ESG investors easily funding dete hain.
    *   *Risk Mitigation:* Kanooni penalties, public shaming, aur pipeline failures se protection milti hai.
