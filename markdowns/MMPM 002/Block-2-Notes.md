### UNIT 4: COMMUNICATION SKILLS

#### 1. Objectives of Communication in Sales
*   Every sales call is an exercise in persuasive communication. The key objectives are:
    1.  *Laying Credibility:* Establishing trust through expertise (product knowledge) and relationships (trustworthiness).
    2.  *Framing Goals:* Finding common ground and positioning the product to solve customer-specific problems.
    3.  *Compelling Evidence:* Using vivid language, stories, metaphors, and numbers to make the presentation memorable.
    4.  *Emotional Connection:* Showing passion for the product and connecting with the buyer's underlying feelings.
*   **Active Listening:** The cornerstone of sales communication. Active listening means listening to understand, not just to respond. It involves paraphrasing, pausing, and asking questions to confirm engagement.

#### 2. Verbal vs. Written Communication in Selling
*   **Differences:**
| Feature | Written Communication | Verbal/Oral Communication |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Purpose** | Analytical & Reasoning (complex data) | Assimilative & Emotive (building rapport) |
| **Audience** | Non-Interactive (one-way message) | Interactive (two-way dialogue) |
| **Medium** | Frozen (text, brochures, letters) | Fluid (voice tone, words, expressions) |
| **Feedback** | Delayed or None | Quick & Immediate |
| **Flexibility** | Rigid, permanent record | Highly flexible and adjustable on the spot |
| **Cost** | High (printing, drafting) | Low (direct conversation) |

*   *Written Application:* Sales proposals, brochures, and quotation letters must be brief, focused, and data-backed.
*   *Verbal Application:* Real-time pitch, handling objections, and customer interaction.

#### 3. Relevance of Non-Verbal Communication
*   **Impact:** Research suggests that over **70% of the impact** of verbal communication comes from non-verbal cues (body language, tone, gestures, physical space).
*   **Key Non-Verbal Cues:**
    *   *Posture:* Sitting upright indicates professionalism and interest; slouching conveys a casual, uncaring attitude.
    *   *Eye Contact:* Builds trust. Avoid staring or looking away, which suggests dishonesty.
    *   *Voice Modulation:* Pitch, speed, and volume affect customer confidence. Slower speech indicates control, while rapid speech can seem pushy.
    *   *Proxemics:* Maintaining an appropriate physical distance (personal space) so the customer does not feel crowded.

#### 4. Telecommunication and Remote Communication in Modern Sales
*   **Telecommunication (Phone Etiquette):**
    *   Prepare content before dialing; keep it crisp.
    *   Verify the receiver's identity immediately ("Am I speaking with Mr. X?").
    *   Do not put customers on hold without explaining why.
    *   End with pleasant terms (e.g., "Nice speaking with you"); avoid abrupt hang-ups.
*   **Remote Communication (Virtual Sales):**
    *   *Synchronous:* Real-time video meetings (Zoom, MS Teams). Requires professional background, checked audio/video feed, and alert posture.
    *   *Asynchronous:* Pre-recorded demo videos or follow-up emails shared for later viewing.

### UNIT 5: NEGOTIATION SKILLS

#### 1. Defining Negotiation and Distinguishing it from Selling
*   **Negotiation:** A process of resolving conflicts where two or more parties modify their demands to reach a mutually acceptable compromise.
*   **Selling vs. Negotiating:**
    *   *Selling:* The process of identifying customer needs and persuading them to accept a product or service.
    *   *Negotiating:* The process of agreeing on the terms of the transaction (price, delivery, credit) once the buyer has shown interest. Selling builds the desire; negotiating settles the transaction terms.

#### 2. The Negotiating Continuum
*   Negotiation involves movement along a range from each party's ideal position to a mutually acceptable settlement point.
```
Seller's Ideal (Max Price) ◄──────────────────────► Customer's Ideal (Min Price)
                                 ▲
                          Settlement Point
                         (Mutually Agreeable)
```
*   The relative bargaining power and skills of the negotiators determine where the settlement point lands.

#### 3. Steps of the Negotiation Process
1.  **Prepare:** Define and prioritize realistic objectives. Gather information on buyer attitudes and prepare a flexible strategy.
2.  **Discussion:** Explore needs and limits. Avoid interrupting, using sarcasm, or talking too much.
3.  **Signaling:** Drop subtle hints of willingness to move from ideal positions. Listen closely for the opponent's signals.
4.  **Proposing:** Formulate tentative proposals. Keep proposals conditional ("If you buy 100 units, then we can offer a 5% discount").
5.  **Packaging the Offer:** Bundle price, delivery, warranty, and payment terms together to meet the buyer's objectives.
6.  **Bargaining:** Trade concessions. *Never give away a concession without asking for something in return.*
7.  **Closing and Agreeing:** Identify the final point to stop trading. Document the agreement in detail immediately.
8.  **Handling Deadlock:** If negotiations stall, change negotiators, suggest a recess, or break the problem into smaller parts.

#### 4. Negotiation Strategies
*   **Strike Back:** Responding aggressively to buyer pressure. This can damage relationships and should be used with caution.
*   **Give In:** Acceding to customer demands to secure the deal. This hurts profit margins and sets a bad precedent.
*   **Break Off:** Walking away when terms are unacceptable. Validates your position and prevents unprofitable deals.

#### 5. Principles of Sales Negotiations
*   Everything must be conditional ("If you... then we...").
*   Keep the entire package in mind; do not negotiate items individually.
*   Start with a high but realistic opening position to leave room for concessions.
*   Listen more than you talk; information is power.

### UNIT 6: MERCHANDISING AND MANAGING SALES DISPLAYS

#### 1. Merchandising and Visual Merchandising (VM)
*   **Merchandising:** Displaying and presenting products at the retail level to maximize sales.
*   **Visual Merchandising:** Designing the retail store space, product layout, and display materials to attract customer attention, educate them, and encourage buying.
*   **Role of the Salesperson in VM:**
    *   *Silent Salesperson:* Good display informs and guides customers, reducing the need for sales staff.
    *   *Preventing the Boomerang Effect:* Aggressive sales staff can irritate customers and drive them away. Proper displays let customers browse privately with visual cues.
    *   *Feedback Provider:* Salespeople observe how customers navigate store displays and provide layout feedback to visual merchandisers.

#### 2. Store Design Basics
*   **Exterior Design (Storefront):** The first impression (signage, entrance, window display). Its primary goal is to turn passers-by into store entrants.
*   **Interior Design:** Floor layout, lighting, fixtures, and checkout counters. It should guide the customer's path smoothly through the merchandise.

#### 3. Key Design Principles of Sales Displays
*   **Unity:** All elements (signs, products, props) must look organized and work together as a single concept, avoiding clutter.
*   **Balance:**
    *   *Symmetrical (Formal):* Equal visual weight on both sides of a center line (organized, stable).
    *   *Asymmetrical (Informal):* Dissimilar elements with equal visual weight (dynamic, interesting).
*   **Contrast:** Highlighting a product using contrasting colors, sizes, or light settings (e.g., light items on a dark background).
*   **Emphasis / Dominance:** Using an oversized item, color block, or special effect to draw immediate focus to a specific display.
*   **Repetition:** Recurring patterns or colors to improve recall and create an attractive background.
*   **Proportion:** Visual harmony where the size of the elements corresponds naturally with each other and the overall store environment.
*   **Color Blocking (Vertical/Horizontal):** Categorizing and arranging products by color (e.g., warm colors like red/yellow vs. cool colors like blue/green) to guide the shopper's eye.

#### 4. Types of Displays (with Examples)
*   **Assortment Display:**
    *   *Open:* Customers touch and try products (e.g., books at Crossword, clothing at Shoppers Stop).
    *   *Closed:* Items are behind glass for security or hygiene (e.g., jewelry, expensive watches at Titan).
*   **Theme Display:** Centered on an event or season (e.g., Diwali themes, bridal displays at saree shops, Christmas decorations).
*   **Coordinated/Ensemble Display:** Suggestive selling by showing related products together (e.g., a mannequin dressed in matching shirt, pants, tie, shoes, and bag).
*   **Unit Display:** Identical items arranged together in a structured layout (e.g., racks of identical coffee mugs or spoons).
*   **Classification Dominance:** Showing a large width and depth of a product line arranged vertically on walls.
*   **Dump Bins / Cut Cases:** Loose items placed in bins for self-service and low-price appeal (common in supermarkets like D-Mart).
