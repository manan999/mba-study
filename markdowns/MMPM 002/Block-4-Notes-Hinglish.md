### Unit 11: Sales Planning, Forecasting and Budgeting

#### 1. Sales Planning Process (Sales Planning ki Process)
Sales planning sales objectives set karne, market opportunities identify karne aur revenue targets achieve karne ke liye zaroori activities ko plan karne ki process hai. Yeh **MOST** (Mission, Objectives, Strategies, Tactics) framework ke under ek three-stage loop ki tarah kaam karta hai:

```
[ Stage 1: Sales Forecasting & Budgeting ]
                  │
                  ▼
[ Stage 2: Territory Design & Allocation ]
                  │
                  ▼
[ Stage 3: Setting Quotas & Performance Standards ]
                  │
                  └─► (Control & Re-forecasting ke liye feedback loop)
```

*   **Strategic vs. Tactical Sales Planning:**
    *   *Strategic Planning:* Long-term resources lagana (e.g., sales force size decide karna, naye geographic markets me enter hona).
    *   *Tactical Planning:* Short-term execution plans (e.g., product-wise monthly sales targets, customer-wise call routing plans).

#### 2. Key Terms in Sales Demand Estimation (Demand Estimation ke Terms)
Market demand ke alag-alag levels ke beech ke differences ko samajhna zaroori hai:
*   **Market Potential (Bazaar ki Kshamta):** Kisi specific market me, ideal marketing efforts ke under, ek *poori industry* ke products/services ki maximum expected sales.
*   **Sales Potential (Company ki Kshamta):** Ideal conditions ke under, ek *individual company* market potential ka jo maximum share achieve karne ki umeed rakh sakti hai.
*   **Sales Forecast (Sales ka Anumaan):** Ek *proposed marketing plan* aur realistic conditions ke under, ek company aane wale time me jitni sales (value ya units) achieve karne ki expectation rakhti hai.
*   **Sales Quota / Target (Sales Quota):** Kisi sales unit (e.g., salesperson, branch) ko target meet karne aur evaluate karne ke liye diya gaya quantitative standard.

#### 3. Sales Forecasting Techniques (Forecasting ke Tarike)

| Method Category | Forecasting Technique | Operational Description | Pros (Fayde) | Cons (Nuksan) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Qualitative / Judgmental** | **Executive Opinion** | Sales, finance aur marketing ke senior executives ka group consensus. | Bohot fast hota hai, managers ke experience aur intuition ka use hota hai. | Groupthink ho sakta hai (bina soche haan kehna), statistics ka use nahi hota. |
| | **Sales Force Composite** | Grassroots approach jisme har salesperson apne territory ki expected sales ka estimate deta hai, jise baad me sum kiya jata hai. | Customers ke sath direct connect ka fayda milta hai; reps motivate hote hain. | Reps low estimates de sakte hain taaki quota easy mile. |
| | **Delphi Method** | Anonymous experts successive rounds me questionnaires ka answer dete hain. Coordinator responses ko summarize karke common consensus banata hai. | Ego clashes aur dominant personalities ka pressure khatam hota hai. | Time-consuming hai; cooperative external experts chahiye. |
| | **Survey of Buyer Intentions** | Direct customer survey jisme unse pucha jata hai ki aane wale time me unke purchase plans kya hain. | B2B industrial markets me highly accurate jahan buyers limited hote hain. | Consumer markets me kam reliable kyunki customers mind change karte rehte hain. |
| **Quantitative / Statistical** | **Moving Averages** | Pichle kuch fixed periods ke sales average ka use karke agle period ka projection nikalna. | Calculate karna simple hai; random ups-downs ko smooth karta hai. | Slow trends ya sudden fluctuations ko catch nahi karta; purane data ko equal weight deta hai. |
| | **Exponential Smoothing** | Weighted moving average jisme check calculations me recent data ko zyada importance (weight) diya jata hai using constant ($\alpha$). | Kam historical data chahiye; recent market changes par quickly respond karta hai. | Best smoothing constant ($\alpha$) select karna calculation trial-error method hai. |
| | **Regression Analysis** | Sales (dependent variable) aur economic indicators/marketing budget (independent variables) ke beech mathematical relation set karna. | Cause-and-effect relationship explain karta hai; statistically unbiased hai. | Complex hai; reliable external indicators data chahiye. |
| **Experimental** | **Market Testing** | Naye product ko kisi limited geographic area (test market) me launch karke consumer buying behavior check karna. | Actual buyer action data milta hai; new product launch ke liye highly reliable. | Bohot cost lagti hai, time lagta hai, aur competitors ko pehle pata chal jata hai. |

#### 4. Sales Forecasting for New Products (Naye Products ke Liye Forecasting)
Naye product category ke liye forecasting karna mushkil hota hai kyunki **purana data (historical data) nahi hota**. Recommended methods niche diye gaye hain:
1.  **Market Testing:** Cities me pilot test run karke trial rate aur repeat buying patterns check karna.
2.  **Survey of Buyer Intentions:** Focus groups aur prototype demonstrations ke through product acceptance level check karna.
3.  **Analogous Product Method:** Milte-julte purane launched products ke historical growth curve se pattern copy karna.
4.  **Delphi Method:** Naye market trends aur adopt rates predict karne ke liye industry expert panels ka coordination use karna.

#### 5. Role & Importance of Forecasting in Sales Planning
*   **Production & Inventory Control:** Stockout (maal khatam hona) ya excess inventory (faltu stock jama hona) se bachata hai.
*   **Budgetary Allocation:** Cash requirements aur departments ke expense budgets clear karta hai.
*   **Salesforce Management:** Territory design, quota allocation aur sales force sizing me help karta.
*   **Recession Planning (Mandi me planning):** Recession ke time, accurate forecasting se management resilient segments ko target kar sakti hai, resources optimal use kar sakti hai aur defensive actions le sakti hai.

#### 6. Sales Budgeting & Phasing
*   **Sales Budget:** Ek financial plan jo expected sales revenue aur usko achieve karne ke liye zaroori costs (salaries, commissions, travel expenses, advertisements) ko outline karta hai.
*   **Bottom-Up vs. Top-Down Budgeting:**
    *   *Top-Down:* Head office directly budget set karke branch ko deta hai (fast but unrealistic ho sakta hai).
    *   *Bottom-Up:* Field supervisors budget details banakar HQ approve karne bhejte hain (realistic but slow process).
*   **Budget Phasing:** Annual budget ko monthly/quarterly sections me divide karna taaki seasonal fluctuations ko manage kiya ja sake.
*   **Budgetary Control & Deviations:** Actual sales aur actual expenses ko budgeted figures ke sath monitor karna. **Deviation (Antar)** operational gaps highlight karta hai:
    *   *Positive sales deviation* successful marketing run show karta hai.
    *   *Negative sales deviation / over-budget cost* margins protect karne ke liye immediate control actions trigger karta hai.

### Unit 12: Territory Management and Sales Quotas

#### 1. Sales Territories (Sales Territory)
*   **Definition:** Ek unique salesperson ko assigned customer base, industry type, ya geographic locations range.
*   **Territory banana kyun zaroori hai (Goals):**
    1.  **Proper Market Coverage:** Har single potential customer tak systematically touchpoints maintain karna.
    2.  **Effective Salesforce Deployment:** Rep ki competence aur territory requirement ke match par staff positioning karna.
    3.  **Efficient Customer Service:** Long term relations aur regular customer visits cycles standardise karna.
    4.  **Objective Evaluation:** Reps ka assessment unke territory potential ke scope me baseline verify karke karna.
    5.  **Improving Productivity:** Travel time optimize karna, expense costs control karna aur local promotions support improve karna.

#### 2. Scientific Steps in Territory Planning (Scientific Steps)
Territory design systematically 5 steps follow karta hai:

```
[ Step 1: Salesperson ki Capacity measure karein ]
                       │
                       ▼
[ Step 2: Customer Call Frequency set karein (A/B/C) ]
                       │
                       ▼
[ Step 3: Sales Potential me Parity / Equality layein ]
                       │
                       ▼
[ Step 4: Travel Time minimize karein (Territory Shape design) ]
                       │
                       ▼
[ Step 5: Salesperson ka Headquarters fix karein ]
```

*   **Step 1: Salesperson's Capacity:** Ek rep monthly kitne visits control kar sakta hai (e.g., 8 calls/day $\times$ 25 working days = 200 call capacity).
*   **Step 2: Customer Call Frequency:** Accounts classification:
    *   *Class A (VIP/High potential):* Month me 2 baar calls.
    *   *Class B (Medium potential):* Month me 1 baar call.
    *   *Class C (Low potential):* 2 mahine me 1 baar call.
*   **Step 3: Parity in Potential:** Dono territories me potential lagbhag equal hona chahiye taaki direct performance comparison fair ho. (e.g., agar ek territory ka potential ₹10 Lakh hai aur dusri ka ₹5 Lakh, toh direct sales targets compare karna wrong hai).
*   **Step 4: Minimize Travel Time (Shapes):**
    *   *Circle Shape:* Customer density uniform hone par. Headquarters center me hota hai. FMCG aur Pharma sales me common hai.
    *   *Cloverleaf Shape:* Customers scatter hone par. Rep har leaf week loop par travel karta hai aur weekend par central base wapas aata hai. Industrial goods line me useful.
    *   *Wedge Shape:* Urban core zone ko adjacent rural segments se merge karne par radius distribution outline banana.
*   **Step 5: Stationing (Headquarters):** Headquarter town wahan hona chahiye jahan client density maximum hai taaki lodging aur transit costs reduce hon.

#### 3. Approaches to Territory Design
*   **Market Build-Up Approach:**
    1.  Geographic segment unit me saare present aur potential clients control data note karein.
    2.  Client segment call frequency specifications multiply karke required call requirements load compute karein.
    3.  Un control units ko combine karke equal potential workload territories outline karein.
    *   *Usage:* B2B industrial setups me jahan customer database exact available hota hai.
*   **Workload Approach (Talley's Method):**
    1.  Clients ko size scale (sales volume) par segment karein.
    2.  Class frequency standards set karein.
    3.  Geographic control maps define karein.
    4.  Total required annual calls measure karein.
    5.  Reps ke workload (number of calls) ko balance karke layout finalize karein.
    *   *Limitations:* New account opportunities growth scale neglect ho jati hai aur transit terrain details standardise karna tough hota hai.

#### 4. Account Prioritization & Profitability Models
*   **Single-Factor Model:** Sirf volume/value ke direct standard par client ko A, B, C range allocate karna.
*   **Portfolio Model:** Matrix classification system: **Account Opportunity** (growth) vs. **Competitive Position** (firm's power).

```
                      HIGH  |------------------------|------------------------|
                            |        SEGMENT 1       |        SEGMENT 2       |
                            |      High Opportunity  |      High Opportunity  |
                            |      Weak Position     |     Strong Position    |
                            |    (Build strength)    |   (Commit resources)   |
        ACCOUNT             |------------------------|------------------------|
      OPPORTUNITY           |        SEGMENT 3       |        SEGMENT 4       |
                            |      Low Opportunity   |      Low Opportunity   |
                            |      Weak Position     |     Strong Position    |
                            |      (Divest/Drop)     |   (Maintain/Harvest)   |
                       LOW  |------------------------|------------------------|
                                                LOW                     HIGH
                                            COMPETITIVE POSITION
```

*   **Decision Model:** Customer sales response ko study karke call allocations curve adjust karna.
*   **Sales Process Model:** Funnel monitoring system: Open Opportunities $\rightarrow$ Qualified Opportunities $\rightarrow$ Best Opportunities.
*   **Sales & Profit Dilemma (Cost per Call):**
    High sales scale hone par bhi net loss ho sakta hai agar calling costs high hon:
    $$\text{Average Cost per Call} = \frac{\text{Salary / CTC of Salesperson} + \text{Direct Travel/Lodging Expenses}}{\text{Net Active Selling Days} \times \text{Average Calls per Day}}$$
    *   **Breakeven Sales Volume:** Woh minimum sale value jo call cost recover kare:
        $$\text{Breakeven Sales Value} = \frac{\text{Cost of Sales Call}}{\text{Salesforce Cost as a \% of Sales}}$$

#### 5. Sales Quota Systems
*   **Types of Quotas:**
    *   *Sales Volume Quota:* Direct target in rupees, quantity units ya point score metrics.
    *   *Financial Quota:* Net profit margin targets set karna (taaki high margin goods push hon) ya expense control thresholds track karna.
    *   *Activity Quota:* Inputs track karna (demos count, complaints solved, payments collection visits).
    *   *Weighted Combination Quota:* Alag parameters (Volume + Demos + Collections) ka weighted score points.
*   **Attributes of a Good Quota Plan (Ache Quota Plan ke features):**
    *   *Realistic Attainability:* Quota practical aur achievable ho.
    *   *Objective Accuracy:* Intuitions ke badle forecast statistics calculations par based ho.
    *   *Ease of Understanding:* Rep ko dynamic simple target clear dikhe.
    *   *Flexibility:* Market shifts (e.g., sudden lockdown) hone par update constraints allow kare.
    *   *Fairness:* New joiners aur tough terrains details baseline adjustments check kare.
*   **Case Analysis: Raising Quotas and Compensation proportionately to address declining market share:**
    *   *Negative Risks:* Agar market fall natural hai, toh unrealistically quota raise karne se demotivation hoga aur retention crash karegi. Reps quick sales ke liye extra discount dumping operations shuru karenge jo brand margins crash kar dega. Fixed costs levels badhne se company ka operational breakeven point up chala jata hai.
    *   *Positive Benefits:* Potential growth market zones me targets push targets achieve karne me stars motivation leverage improve karega.

### Unit 13: Sales Organisation

#### 1. Need and Purpose of a Sales Organization (Need and Purpose)
Sales organization sales policies aur campaigns process coordinate karne ka structure line framework hai:
*   **Specialization:** Niche operations (technical support, training specs, direct key account sales) ko divide karna.
*   **Clear Authority & Communication:** Vertical reporting channels set karna taaki role ambiguity (confusion) na ho.
*   **Coordination:** Price decisions, logistical delivery updates, aur marketing setups coordinate karna.
*   **Span of Control:** Managers ke niche reports limit karna taaki operational activities manage ho sakein.

#### 2. Process of Designing a Sales Organization (Steps)

```
[ Step 1: Corporate & Sales Objectives define karein ]
                            │
                            ▼
[ Step 2: Zaroori Sales Activities identify karein ]
                            │
                            ▼
[ Step 3: Activities ko Levels me group karein ]
                            │
                            ▼
[ Step 4: Specific staff ko positions assign karein ]
                            │
                            ▼
[ Step 5: Authority lines aur Span of Control set karein ]
```

#### 3. Factors Influencing Structure & Size
*   *Company Size & Resources:* Choti companies direct setups use karti hain, badi operations multi-layered structures use karti hain.
*   *Product Diversity:* Broad ya complex technical items hone par product specialisation use hota hai.
*   *Market Coverage:* Market broad geographic scatter hone par regional branches and geographic setups setup hote hain.

#### 4. Basic Organizational Structures (Structures Comparison)

| Structure Type | Operational Concept | Advantages (Fayde) | Disadvantages (Nuksan) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Line Sales Organisation** | Vertical direct chain. Subordinate ek boss ke niche directly review control hota hai. | • Decisions fast hote hain.<br>• Administrative controls simple hain.<br>• Roles structure clear hai. | • Senior executives tasks overload se busy ho jate hain.<br>• Specialisation supports absent hai.<br>• Broad product lines manage nahi hoti. |
| **Line & Staff Sales Organisation** | Line authority to remain, advisory support managers (training, recruiter specialists) addition. | • Line managers execution focus free.<br>• Strategic consulting expertise improvements.<br>• Targeted training programs. | • Overhead budget costs rise.<br>• Conflict potential (staff overstepping commands vs line ignore suggestions). |
| **Functional Sales Organisation** | Reps receive instructions from multiple functional directors simultaneously. | • High specialisation training support. | • Unity of Command template violate hota hai.<br>• Subordinate confusion levels high. |

#### 5. Field Sales Specialization Designs (Specializations Types)
Field structures designs 3 main types me divide hote hain:
1.  **Geographic Specialisation:** Regional territories create karna jahan rep saare products ek region me bechta hai. Travel expenses low hote hain aur relationship depth strong hoti hai.
2.  **Product Specialisation:** Specialized product managers and separate product lines setup karna. Technical queries details solutions are accurate, but overlapping customer travel counts occur (duplication).
3.  **Customer / Market Specialisation:** Client vertical segmentation check target (e.g., government clients manager, corporate clients team). Client expectations understand patterns improve, but admin complexity high rehti hai.

#### 6. Determining Sales Force Size (Sales Force Size Calculations)
Size calculate karne ke standard 3 mathematical ways hain:
1.  **Breakdown Method:**
    $$N = \frac{\text{Total Forecasted Sales Volume}}{\text{Estimated Productivity of One Salesperson}}$$
2.  **Workload Method:**
    $$N = \frac{\text{Total Number of Calls Required per Year}}{\text{Average Annual Calls One Salesperson Can Perform}}$$
3.  **Incremental Method:**
    Tab tak reps count badhate jaayein jab tak new rep ki marginal profit contribution ($MP$) us rep ki marginal cost ($MC$) se high ho:
    $$\text{Add Rep if: } MP > MC$$

### Unit 14: Sales Control, Analysis and Sales Audit

#### 1. Sales Control: Meaning and Purpose
Sales control targets standard set karna, actual performances evaluate karna, deviation compute karna aur control policies update actions deploy karna hai. Iska main aim budget misuse check karna aur underperforming segments ko save karna hai.

#### 2. The Sales Control System (Steps)
```
[ Step 1: Standards Set karein (e.g. Quota targets) ]
                            │
                            ▼
[ Step 2: Actuals details measure karein (MIS reports) ]
                            │
                            ▼
[ Step 3: Compare actuals with standard and spot Deviations ]
                            │
                            ▼
[ Step 4: Corrective Actions deploy karein (MBE rules) ]
```
*   **Management by Exception (MBE):** Chote anomalies deviations ko ignore karke management focus exclusively major deviations points par deploy karna.

#### 3. Core Methods of Sales Control (Control Methods)

#### A. Sales Analysis
*   **80-20 Rule (Pareto Principle):** Companies me generally 80% revenue select 20% products, regions, or customer accounts se aata hai.
*   **Iceberg Principle:** Aggregated high-level sales data true problems mask karta hai. Jaise iceberg ka 10% hi bahar dikhta hai aur 90% pani ke niche chupa rehta hai. Branch target 100% complete showing ke peeche 5 products failure hidden ho sakte hain.
*   *Gulani Case analysis:* Rep Gulani ka overall target achieved shows 82%. Depth check reveals desk computer and laptop metrics achieved targets were 100%+, but Xerox machine performance was 20% due to loss of one government customer account (which was 80% of Xerox target allocation).

#### B. Marketing Cost Analysis
*   **Usefulness:** Segment (territories, SKU sizes) profitability trace karna, minimum order size value check point maintain karna, promotion designs output measure check.
*   **Approaches:**
    *   *Full-Cost Approach:* Variable and fixed overheads common allocations process dynamically apply. Complete net profits calculations are possible, but allocations logic subjective standard is contentious.
    *   *Contribution-Margin Approach:* Only variable direct cost allocations are run, leaving general corporate overhead structures unallocated.

#### C. Sales Management Audit
*   **Objectives:** Comprehensive strategic check. Strategy assumptions check, future expansion options outline benchmarks validation.
*   **Audit Parameters:** Quota methods review, call frequency ratios check, CAC values evaluate, onboarding workflows.
*   **Criticism:** Historical audit records are reactive templates. Volatile dynamic trends me future safety assure nahi hoti.
