## Unit 4: Product Decisions

### 1. Core Service Product Concept (Kotler ke 5 Levels)
* **Core Benefit**: Wo main benefit ya utility jo customer khareed raha hai (e.g., Hotel: rest/sleep).
* **Basic Product**: Wo fundamental, functional attributes jo zaroori hain (e.g., Hotel: room, bed, bath).
* **Expected Product**: Attributes ya conditions jo buyer normally expect karta hai (e.g., Hotel: clean sheets, quietness).
* **Augmented Product**: Wo features jo customer expectations ko exceed karte hain aur competitors se differentiate karte hain (e.g., Hotel: prompt room service, complimentary breakfast).
* **Potential Product**: Service ka future evolution jo future me offering ko aur behtar banayega (e.g., Hotel: all-suite concept, personalized smart-room preferences).

### 2. Gronroos ke Three Levels of Service Product
1. **Basic Service Package**: Core service + Facilitating services (jo delivery ke liye mandatory hain, jaise check-in desk) + Supporting services (jo value add karti hain, jaise health club).
2. **Augmented Service Offering**: Package ko expand karna jisme *accessibility* (working hours, location), *interaction* (physical/technical resources ke sath), aur *customer participation* shaamil hote hain.
3. **Market Communication**: Brand image ko promotions ke through manage karna taaki customers value aur augmentation ko recognize kar sakein.

### 3. Stages in New Service Development (NSD) (Nayi Service Develop Karne ki Stages)
Ek nayi service ko market me lane ke liye, managers paanch stages se guzarte hain:
1. **Customer Benefit Concept**: Customer needs ko expected benefits me translate karna.
2. **Service Concept**: Specific benefit path ko define karna aur is sawaal ka jawab dena, "Hum kis business me hain?" (e.g., entertainment vs. fine arts).
3. **Service Offer**: Intangible elements (performances, scripts) aur tangible infrastructure (facilities, seating) dono ki details ko complete karna.
4. **Service Forms**: Delivery options aur packages ko select karna (e.g., yearly membership fees vs. single tickets).
5. **Service Delivery System**: People (competencies) aur physical evidence (facilitating goods) ko structure karna jo service perform karenge.

### 4. Service Positioning & Positioning Strategies
* **Service Positioning**: Company ki offering aur image ko target market ke mind me ek distinctive (alag) jagah dilane ke liye design karna. Isme **3 Cs** (Customer, Competitor, Company) aur **STP** (Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning) ka use hota hai.
* **Ries and Trout ke Positioning Options**:
  1. *Strengthening current position*: Competitors ke khilaf apni position ko strong karna (bina direct clash ke apni offerings sudharna).
  2. *Identifying an unoccupied market position*: Market ke gaps ko fill karna jahan koi competitor nahi hai.
  3. *Repositioning the competition*: Active promotion/innovation ke through competitors ko piche chhodna.
* **Positioning Strategies / Themes**:
  - *Positioning by Attributes*: Kisi unique feature par differentiate karna (e.g., theme-based resorts).
  - *Positioning by Benefits*: Specific customer rewards par focus karna (e.g., online platforms par bank-associated discounts).
  - *Positioning by Competition*: Directly competitor ke sath compare karna.
  - *Price-Based Positioning*: Price ke relative me value/prestige ko emphasize karna.
  - *Positioning as a Leader*: Sector me #1 hone ka fayda uthana (e.g., life insurance me LIC).
  - *User Positioning*: Specific target groups ke liye customize karna (e.g., IRCTC ke special senior citizen pilgrimage packages).
  - *Use/Application Positioning*: Kisi specific application ke liye position karna (e.g., bank ka khud ko "best home loan provider" batana).

## Unit 5: Pricing Decisions

### 1. Pricing Goods vs. Services (Pricing me Antar)
Physical goods ke मुकाबले services ki pricing bohot complex hoti hai, kyunki:
* **Costing Difficulties**: Services me physical raw materials nahi hote. Costs ko input units (e.g., time, personnel hours) ke basis par calculate kiya jata hai.
* **Intangibility**: High intangibility ke karan non-uniform, negotiated pricing hoti hai (e.g., consultancies, legal fees).
* **Perishability**: Demand fluctuations ko inventory se buffer nahi kiya ja sakta, isliye dynamic price adjustments zaroori hote hain.
* **Price Regulations**: Bohot si services (electricity, rail fares, bank base charges) government-controlled hoti hain.
* **Pricing Terminology**: Terminology industry ke hisab se vary karti hai:
  - *Premium* (Insurance), *Interest* (Use of money), *Fare* (Transport), *Fee* (Education, Health), *Rent* (Property usage), *Commission* (Brokerage), *Tariff* (Utilities), *Toll* (Road usage).

### 2. Role of Non-Monetary Costs (Non-Monetary Costs ka Role)
Customers total sacrifice ke basis par purchase evaluate karte hain, jisme ye costs shaamil hain:
* **Time Costs**: Travel karne me, wait karne me, aur service ke sath interact karne me jo time spend hota hai.
* **Search Costs**: Intangible options ko research, locate, aur compare karne ki mehnat.
* **Psychic Costs**: Psychological friction jaise rejection ka darr (e.g., loan approvals) ya complex systems use karne ki anxiety (e.g., online trading).
* *Strategic Implication*: Agar marketers non-monetary costs ko kam kar dein, toh wo monetary price badha sakte hain.

### 3. The Pricing Tripod
Pricing strategy in teen analytical legs par tika hota hai:
1. **Cost to the Firm**: Ye *price floor* (minimum acceptable price) decide karta hai. Overheads allocate karne ke liye activity-based costing (ABC) use hoti hai.
2. **Value to the Customer**: Ye *price ceiling* (maximum willingness to pay based on perceived utility) decide karta hai.
3. **Competitors' Pricing**: Floor aur ceiling ke beech price ko kahan position karna hai, usme guide karta hai.

### 4. Key Service Pricing Strategies
* **Discount Pricing**: Low-demand seasons me prices kam karna taaki demand shift ho sake.
* **Psychological Pricing**: Odd numbers (e.g., INR 499) par price set karna taaki bargain lage.
* **Penetration Pricing**: Market share capture karne ke liye initial price low rakhna (e.g., Jio launch).
* **Prestige/Skimming Pricing**: Premium segments ke liye high initial price set karna jo status symbol bane.
* **Price Bundling**: Interrelated services ko ek package me bechna (e.g., Amazon Prime bundling shipping and video streaming). Types: *pure bundling* (sirf package me milega), *mixed bundling* (sath me ya alag bhi mil sakta hai), aur *add-on bundling* (base price + extras).
* **Diversionary / Offset Pricing**: Low basic price se customer ko attract karna, fir post-delivery ya peripheral services par high rates charge karna.
* **Guaranteed Pricing**: Payout tabhi lena jab results deliver ho jayein (e.g., headhunters, real estate brokers).
* **Captive / Two-Part Pricing**: Entry level basic price low rakhna, aur usage/refills par ongoing fees lena (e.g., DTH setup box + monthly subscription).

* **Differential/Dynamic Pricing Case (IRCTC Tatkal vs. Premium Tatkal)**:
  - *Tatkal*: Basic fare par ek fixed additional percentage (10% to 30%) charge karta hai jo defined floor/ceiling ke andar hota hai.
  - *Premium Tatkal*: Dynamic/surge pricing use karta hai, jahan seat availability kam hone aur booking speed badhne par fares automatically badhte hain.
  - *Pros of Dynamic Pricing*: High-urgency travelers se revenue maximize hota hai; peak capacity manage hoti hai.
  - *Cons*: Agar transparency na ho, toh customer dissatisfaction aur unfairness ka perception badhta hai.

## Unit 6: Place Decisions

### 1. Modes of Service Distribution (Distribution ke Tareeqe)
1. **Customers Visit the Service Site**: Physical convenient outlets zaroori hain (e.g., hospitals, cafes, universities).
2. **Service Providers Go to Customers**: Site-specific services ke liye zaroori hai (e.g., pest control, catering, home painting).
3. **Transactions Conducted Remotely**: Delivery digital channels se door se hi hoti hai (e.g., online banking, stock trading, SaaS).

### 2. Channels of Service Distribution
* **Direct Sales**: Provider se seedhe customer tak (e.g., online courses). Isme quality control aur customer feedback maximum hota hai.
  - *Electronic Channels*: 24/7 convenience, consistent delivery, aur low cost (e.g., online flight ticketing).
* **Sales via Intermediaries (Intermediaries ke through sales)**: Tab use hota hai jab direct reach geographically restricted ho:
  - **Franchising**: Royalties ke badle me trademark, business format ya method use karne ka right dena (e.g., fast food chains). Kam capital risk me fast expansion promote karta hai.
  - **Quasi-Retailing**: Aise outlets jo physical inventory ki jagah intangible services sell karte hain (e.g., travel agencies, hair salons, bank branches).
    - *Strategic tips*: Complementary facilities ke paas locate karein, aur back-end production ko centralize karke customer contact points ko decentralize karein (e.g., diagnostic labs).
  - **Agents & Brokers**: 
    - *Agents*: Principal ko long-term commission-based contract par represent karte hain (e.g., insurance agents).
    - *Brokers*: Buyer aur seller ko ek specific transaction ke liye ek sath laate hain (e.g., real estate brokers).

## Unit 7: Promotion Decisions

### 1. Challenges in Service Communications (Mittal's 5 Problems of Intangibility)
* **Incorporeal Existence (Physical form na hona)**: Services physical nahi hoti, isliye display karna mushkil hai.
  - *Strategy*: **Physical Representation** use karein (jaise premium aircraft seats dikhana).
* **Abstractness (Amurtata)**: Financial safety ya health jaise concepts ko visualize karna mushkil hai.
  - *Strategy*: **Service Consumption Episode** dikhayein (customers ko service benefits enjoy karte hue dikhana).
* **Generality**: Vague promises ("great quality") differentiation nahi banati.
  - *Strategy*: **System Documentation** (facts, figures, capacity dikhana) ya **Performance Documentation** (purani success rates dikhana) use karein.
* **Non-Searchability**: Purchase se pehle check nahi kiya ja sakta.
  - *Strategy*: **Independent Performance Documentation** (audits) aur **Consumption Documentation** (customer testimonials) use karein.
* **Mental Impalpability (Samajh me na aana)**: Complex services ko samajhna naye logo ke liye mushkil hota.
  - *Strategy*: **Service Process Episode** (step-by-step delivery process dikhana) ya **Case History Episode** (customer ki actual case study) dikhayein.

### 2. Guidelines for Advertising Campaigns (PRO Perspectives)
Kisi prestigious educational/management institution ke liye ad campaign banate waqt ye guidelines follow karein:
* **Positive Staff Impact**: Ad aisa ho jo internal faculty aur staff ko promises deliver karne ke liye motivate kare.
* **Provide Tangible Clues**: Physical evidence dikhayein (campus infrastructure, digital library, class interactions).
* **Make Service Understandable**: Learning process aur curriculum flexibility ko clearly define karein.
* **Capitalize on Word-of-Mouth**: Successful alumni aur corporate recruiters ke authentic testimonials add karein.
* **Continuity**: Consistent symbols, themes, aur institutional colors use karein.
* **Promise Only the Possible**: Credibility banaye rakhne ke liye placement records ko badha-chadhakar na dikhayein.

### 3. Methods of Service Promotion (Promotion Mix)
* **Advertising**: Services ko tangibilize karne aur brand familiarity build karne par focus karta hai.
* **Sales Promotion**: Goods ke मुकाबले limited hota hai (sampling mehenga hai). Isme *premiums* (physical gifts to add tangibility) aur *price/quantity deals* (group travel discounts) par focus hota hai.
* **Personal Selling**: High interaction; isme personal relationships build karna, professional competence dikhana, aur packages customize karna zaroori hai.
* **Public Relations & Publicity**: Community outreach aur media stories (e.g., doctors ka free rural health camps lagana). Highly credible aur cost-effective hota hai.
* **Social Media & Online Channels**: YouTube, blogs, aur review platforms vital hain. Services experience-heavy hoti hain, isliye peer reviews, viral videos, aur influencer endorsements ("word-of-mouse") customer ke alternative evaluation ke liye powerful triggers hote hain.
* **Integrated Service Marketing Communications (ISMC)**: Saare departments (marketing, HR, operations, PR) ke beech coordination taaki har touchpoint (ad copy, website, frontline interaction) se ek hi, consistent brand message jaye.
