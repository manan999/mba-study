### UNIT 4: METHODS AND TECHNIQUES OF DATA COLLECTION

#### 1. Primary vs. Secondary Data
*   **Primary Data:** Woh data jo bilkul fresh aur pehli baar collect kiya jata hai directly target sources se. Yeh character me original hota hai.
*   **Secondary Data:** Woh data jo pehle hi kisi doosri agency ya organization ne apne kisi purpose ke liye collect aur process kar liya hai.
*   **Context Dependency Metaphor (Situation ka khel):**
    *   *Census Bureau* jo census figures direct collect karta hai; unke liye yeh **Primary Data** hai.
    *   *Ek Demographer* ya academic student jo unhi published census figures ko use karke growth chart banata hai; uske liye yeh **Secondary Data** hai.

#### 2. Comparison of Primary Data Collection Methods
Alag-alag situations, budget, aur time ke hisab se sahi method choose kiya jata hai:

| Method | Description | Merits | Demerits | Preferred When |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Observation** | Researcher khud bina kisi se sawaal puche situation ko observe aur record karta hai. | Objective hota hai, respondent ke jhooth bolne ka chance nahi hota, actual behavior record hota hai. | Investigator bias ho sakta hai, scope limited hota hai, expensive hai, thoughts/attitude pata nahi chalte. | Natural behavior studies me (e.g., traffic counts, retail store me shelf visits). |
| **Personal Interview** | Face-to-face sawaal-jawaab karna structured ya unstructured questionnaire ke sath. | Sabse flexible hai, response rate sabse high hota hai, deep details aur gestures note kiye ja sakte hain. | Sabse costly method hai, isme badhiya supervision chahiye, interviewer bias ka chance hota hai. | Complex topics jahan high accuracy aur depth chahiye. |
| **Mailed Questionnaire** | Post ya Email ke through questionnaire bhejna literate respondents ki list ko. | Bohot sasta (cheap) hai, wide geographic coverage milta hai, interviewer bias zero hota hai, respondent anonymous rehta hai. | Non-response rate sabse high hota hai, bohot slow process hai, control nahi hota ki kisne fill kiya, probe nahi kar sakte. | Budget kam ho, time kafi ho, aur target population padhi-likhi (literate) ho. |
| **Telephone Interview** | Phone ke through respondents se sawaal puchna. | Sabse fast method hai, cost kam lagti hai, moderate response rate hota hai, quality monitor karna easy hai. | Chhota (short) interview hi possible hai, jinke paas phone nahi hai unka data nahi aayega, visual aid use nahi kar sakte. | Kam time me simple aur quick feedback lene ke liye. |

#### 3. Secondary Data: Sources, Dangers, and Precautions
*   **Main Sources:** Government publications (e.g., RBI Bulletins, Census reports), trade journals, books, company annual reports, aur commercial databases.
*   **Dangers of Using Secondary Data (Kya khatra hai?):**
    *   *Bias:* Purani agency ne data ko apne kisi biased goal ke liye manipulatively collect kiya ho sakta hai.
    *   *Outdated:* Data purana ho sakta hai jo aaj ki current situation me apply na hota ho.
    *   *Incompatibility:* Definitions, class intervals, ya metrics humare current study se match na karte hon.
    *   *Inaccuracy:* Purani study me sampling errors ya wrong reporting ho sakti hai.
*   **Precautions & Scrutiny (The Three Pillars - Teeno ko check karein):**
    1.  **Reliability:** Kisne data collect kiya tha? Kya unki method scientifically sahi aur unbiased thi?
    2.  **Suitability:** Kya woh data humare current research ke scope, definitions, aur objectives se match karta hai?
    3.  **Adequacy:** Kya data kafi detailed hai, ya bohot summary form me hai jo kaam na aaye?

### UNIT 5: ATTITUDE MEASUREMENT AND SCALES

#### 1. Concept of Attitude & Attitude Measurement
*   **Attitude:** Kisi insaan ke dimag ki woh state (readiness) jo uske experience se banti hai aur uske surrounding objects/situations par reaction ko guide karti hai (cognitive, affective, behavioral components hote hain).
*   **Attitude Measurement:** Qualitative emotions, preferences, aur opinions ko quantitative numbers dena taaki unhe statistically analyze kiya ja sake.

#### 2. The Four Levels of Measurement Scales (Charon Scales)
1.  **Nominal Scale:** Sirf classification ya labeling karna bina kisi relative order ya math value ke (e.g., Gender: Male/Female, State of residence). Isme sirf Mode calculate hota hai.
2.  **Ordinal Scale:** Ranks ya ordered classes banana, par beech ka distance unequal ya unknown hota hai (e.g., Class ranks: 1st, 2nd, 3rd; Product preference ranks). Median isme use hota hai.
3.  **Interval Scale:** Ranks jisme equal distance hota hai, par koi natural absolute zero point nahi hota (e.g., Temperature in Celsius, IQ scores). Mean isme use ho sakta hai.
4.  **Ratio Scale:** Ranks jisme equal distance bhi hota hai aur ek natural absolute zero point bhi hota hai (e.g., Income, Weight, Age, Sales revenue). Saare statistical analyses isme validate hote hain.

#### 3. Essential Scaling Techniques in Research
*   **Thurstone's Equal-Appearing Interval Scale:**
    *   *Method:* Researcher kafi saare attitude statements likhta hai. Panel of "judges" ko bola jata hai ki in statements ko 11 piles me sort karein (Most unfavorable se lekar Most favorable tak).
    *   *Score:* Har statement ka scale value judges ke median rating se decide hota hai. Ambiguous statements ko drop kar dete hain. Final scale me respondents wahi statements tick karte hain jinse woh agree karte hain. Unka score ticked statements ke average/median value se nikalta hai.
    *   *Drawbacks:* Time kafi consume hota hai, judges ke personal attitude se bias aa sakta hai, aur intensity of agreement pata nahi chalti.
*   **Semantic Differential Scale:**
    *   *Method:* Isme 7-point rating scale hoti hai jiske dono ends par opposite (bipolar) adjectives hote hain (e.g., Good vs Bad, Fast vs Slow, Honest vs Dishonest). Beech ke blocks unlabeled hote hain.
    *   *Use:* Brand image profiling me bohot popular hai. Respondents ke average scores ko plot karke competing brands ka comparative profile graph banaya jata hai.
*   **Likert Scale (Summative Model):**
    *   *Method:* Statements ko scale par rate kiya jata hai (usually 5-point: Strongly Agree se Strongly Disagree). Positive items ko 5 se 1 marks dete hain, aur negative items ko reverse (1 se 5) mark kiya jata hai.
    *   *Item Analysis:* Pre-testing ke baad respondents ko high aur low scores me divide kiya jata hai. Jo items high aur low scoring groups ke beech statistically differentiate nahi kar paate, unhe drop kar diya jata hai.
*   **Q-Sort Technique:**
    *   *Method:* Respondents ko cards (jisme statements ya brand names hote hain) diye jaate hain aur unhe fixed piles me sort karne bola jata hai jo normal distribution pattern follow karein (e.g., placing exactly 2 cards in "most liked", 5 in "liked", etc.).
    *   *Use:* Paired comparison se faster hai. Iska main objective clusters of individuals aur intense individual preferences ko study karna hai.
*   **Multidimensional Scaling (MDS):**
    *   *Method:* Mathematical algorithms ke through objects ko unke similarity/preference ke basis par multi-dimensional space me map kiya jata hai.
    *   *Use:* Competitors ke relative product positioning aur market segmentation me iska major use hai.

### UNIT 6: QUESTIONNAIRE DESIGNING

#### 1. The Core Considerations in Questionnaire Construction
Questionnaire primary data collection ka sabse popular tool hai. Ise design karne ke 3 main pillars hain:
1.  **The General Form:** Structured ya Unstructured select karna. Structured questionnaire easy to analyze hote hain par rigid hote hain. Unstructured questionnaires detail me qualitative research ke liye ache hote hain par skilled interviewer chahiye.
2.  **The Question Sequence (Flow):** Sawaalon ka flow logical hona chahiye.
    *   *The Funnel Approach:* Shuruat hamesha simple, interesting aur broad sawaalon se karein taaki rapport build ho sake. Phir specific aur technical sawaalon par jayein.
    *   *Personal Questions:* Age, income, aur sensitive/personal questions ko hamesha questionnaire ke aakhri me rakhein taaki respondent shuru me hi reject na karde.
3.  **The Question Wording:** Sawaalon ke shabda (words) simple, direct aur clear hone chahiye.
    *   *Double-barreled questions avoid karein:* Ek sawaal me do baatein na puchein (e.g., *"Kya aap is brand ke Price aur Quality se khush hain?"*).
    *   *Leading questions avoid karein:* Respondent ko answer guide na karein (e.g., *"Don't you agree ki humara service best hai?"*).

#### 3. The Role of a Pilot Study (Pre-testing)
*   **What is it?** Main survey se pehle questionnaire ko ek chote, representative sample par test run karna.
*   **Why is it used?** Questionnaire ki design flaws, ambiguous words, flow issues, aur layout issues ko catch karne ke liye, taaki real survey me time aur money waste na ho.

### UNIT 7: SAMPLING AND SAMPLING DESIGNS

#### 1. Basic Concepts
*   **Universe/Population:** Woh poora area ya saare elements jinko study karna hai.
*   **Sampling Frame:** Population ke saare elements ki physical list (e.g., company employee list, telephone directory).
*   **Sample:** Population ka ek chota representative hissa (subset) jo actual survey ke liye select kiya jata hai.
*   **Advantages of Sampling over complete census (Census ke upar sampling ke fayde):**
    *   *Kam Cost:* Poori population ka survey karna financially possible nahi hota.
    *   *Zyada Speed:* Data collection aur analysis jaldi poora ho jata hai.
    *   *Destructive Testing:* E.g., blood test ya shelf-life test me sampling hi akela option hai.
    *   *Behtar Accuracy:* Chote group par staff ko achi training aur proper supervision dena easy hota hai, jisse non-sampling errors kam hote hain.

#### 2. Random (Probability) vs. Non-Random (Non-Probability) Sampling
*   **Random Sampling:** Population ke har element ka selection hone ka ek equal aur non-zero chance hota hai. Isme sampling error calculate karna aur generalize karna possible hai.
*   **Non-Random Sampling:** Selection convenience, judgment ya quotas par hota hai. Isme sampling error mathematically measure nahi ho sakta.

#### 3. Probability Sampling Methods (Random Methods)
1.  **Simple Random Sampling (SRS):** Lottery method ya random number table se element choose karna. Isme complete sampling frame zaroori hai.
2.  **Stratified Random Sampling:** Population ko homogenous groups (strata) me divide kiya jata hai (e.g., Income basis: Low, Mid, High), aur har stratum ke andar se SRS lagakar sample liya jata hai. Proportional stratification ratios ko maintain rakhta hai.
3.  **Systematic Sampling:** Sunder list me se har $k$-th element ko select karna ek random start point ke baad.
4.  **Cluster & Multi-stage Sampling:** Population ko heterogeneous groups (clusters, e.g., sectors of city) me divide karna. Randomly clusters select karke, ya toh un clusters ka full survey karna (Cluster) ya unke andar se dubara sample lena (Multi-stage).

#### 4. Non-Probability Sampling Methods (Non-Random Methods)
1.  **Quota Sampling:** Population ko categories me divide karke categories ke andar elements convenience ya interviewer ke judgment ke hisab se select karna.
2.  **Judgment (Purposive) Sampling:** Researcher apne expertise ke basis par un elements ko handpick karta hai jo uske study ke liye sabse suitable hote hain.
3.  **Convenience Sampling:** Jo aasani se mil jaye use select kar lena (e.g., mall intercepts).
4.  **Snowball Sampling:** Initial respondents se reference lekar aage ke respondents tak pohochana (rare/hidden populations ke liye).
*   **When is Non-Random Sampling Suitable?**
    *   **Exploratory studies** jahan high precision zaroori na ho.
    *   Jab **time aur funds ki bohot kami** ho.
    *   Jab population ka **complete sampling frame available na ho**.
    *   Questionnaire testing aur **pilot studies** ke waqt.

#### 5. Case Application: Sampling Design for Domestic Airline Passengers
Agar hume domestic airline passengers ke problems study karne ke liye sample design banana ho, toh uske steps yeh honge:
*   **Target Population:** Kisi selected country me kisi specific time (e.g., 3 months) me fly karne wale saare domestic passengers.
*   **Sampling Unit:** Ek individual passenger.
*   **Sampling Frame:** Passengers ki names sheet confidential hoti hai, isliye **time-space frame** use hoga (e.g., flight schedules, departure gates).
*   **Recommended Sampling Method:**
    *   *First Stage (Stratification):* Flights ko airline brand, route (long vs short haul) aur flight time (morning/night) ke basis par divide (stratify) karein.
    *   *Second Stage (Systematic Sampling):* Selected gates par flight board karte waqt har 5th passenger ko systematically select karein taaki interviewer ka personal bias khatam ho sake.
*   **Sample Size:** Precision limit (margin of error e.g., 3%) aur financial budget ke balance se sample size decide hoga.
