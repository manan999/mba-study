## Unit 11: Managing Service Quality

### 1. Concept of Service Quality (Service Quality ka Concept)
* **Definition**: Customer ki expectations aur unke actual experience/perceptions ke beech ka difference.
  $$\text{Perceived Service Quality} = \text{Perceived Service} - \text{Expected Service}$$
* **Process vs. Outcome**: Customers service quality ko do dimensions par judge karte hain: *technical/outcome quality* (unhe kya mila) aur *functional/process quality* (unhe service kaise mili).

### 2. The SERVQUAL Gaps Model (PZB) Applied to an e-Commerce Portal
Ye model five key gaps ko identify karta hai jo service quality failures ke liye responsible hote hain:
* **Gap 1: The Listening Gap (Customer kya chahta hai, ye na pata hona)**:
  - *e-Commerce context*: Management customer ke search behavior ko research kiye bina hi website categories design kar deta hai.
  - *Solution*: Proactive customer research karna, search logs analyze karna, aur usability testing karna.
* **Gap 2: The Service Design & Standards Gap (Sahi standards set na karna)**:
  - *e-Commerce context*: Management ko pata hai ki customers fast support chahte hain, par wo query tickets ke liye koi response time (SLA) ya page-load speed target set nahi karta.
  - *Solution*: Customer-driven standards set karna, clean user flows design karna, aur fallback chatbots implement karna.
* **Gap 3: The Service Performance Gap (Service standards deliver na kar pana)**:
  - *e-Commerce context*: Flash sale ke dauran website server down ho jana, ya warehouse staff se packaging/billing errors hona, jisse shipping timeline miss ho jaye.
  - *Solution*: High-quality cloud hosting me invest karna, inventory sync ko automate karna, aur operations staff ko behtar train/motivate karna.
* **Gap 4: The Communication Gap (Promises aur performance match na hona)**:
  - *e-Commerce context*: Ad me "Next-Day Delivery across India" ka promise karna lekin systems me remote pin codes exclude na karna, jisse delivery late ho jaye.
  - *Solution*: Marketing campaigns ko company ki actual operational capabilities ke sath align rakhna.
* **Gap 5: The Customer Gap (Expected service aur perceived service ka difference)**:
  - *e-Commerce context*: Gaps 1 to 4 ka net result. Shopper ko lagta hai ki expected delivery speed aur product quality actual experience se match nahi hui.
  - *Solution*: Expected aur perceived service ko align karne ke liye Gaps 1 to 4 ko fill karna.

### 3. Five Dimensions of Service Quality (SERVQUAL) with Hospital Examples
1. **Reliability** (Vaada pura karna):
   - *Hospital Example*: Diagnoses correct hona, billing accurate hona, aur surgeries scheduled time par start aur complete hona.
2. **Responsiveness** (Help karne ki willingness):
   - *Hospital Example*: Call button dabate hi nurses ka turant aana, aur emergency wait time ko minimal rakhna.
3. **Assurance** (Trust aur confidence build karna):
   - *Hospital Example*: Doctors ka professional credentials display karna aur apni deep expertise se patient ka trust jeetna.
4. **Empathy** (Personalized, caring attention):
   - *Hospital Example*: Patients ko unke naam se yaad rakhna, unke darr ko samajhna, aur unke routines ke according treatment schedule plan karna.
5. **Tangibles** (Physical assets aur appearance):
   - *Hospital Example*: Sterile/clean rooms, modern equipment, aur hospital staff ki clean uniforms.

### 4. Causes of Delivery Gaps (Gap 3) in Hospitality & Solutions
* **Causes**:
  * *Role Ambiguity/Conflict*: Staff check-in speed (efficiency) aur hospitality (satisfaction) ke beech confuse rehta hai.
  * *Poor Employee-Job Fit*: High technical skills par low service inclination (interactive/empathy skills) wale log hire karna.
  * *Inadequate Technology/Systems*: Rooms ki cleaning status real-time na dikhna, jisse room allocation me delay ho.
  * *Lack of Teamwork & Empowerment*: Frontline staff ko small issues resolve karne ki authority na hona (e.g., room dirty hone par manager approval ke bina free dinner offer na kar pana).
* **Solutions**:
  * Clear job instructions aur service scripts provide karein.
  * Staff ko technical aur interpersonal dono skills me train karein.
  * Frontline employees ko spot recovery ke liye empower karein.
  * Teamwork encourage karein aur rewards ko customer satisfaction metrics se link karein.

### 5. Advocacy (Positive vs. Negative Word-of-Mouth)
* **Advocacy**: Aise loyal customers jo spontaneously brand ko doosron ko recommend karte hain, jo company ke liye unpaid credible sales force ki tarah kaam karte hain.
* **Net Promoter Score (NPS)**:
  $$\text{NPS} = \% \text{ Promoters (Score 9-10)} - \% \text{ Detractors (Score 0-6)}$$
* **Dynamics of Advocacy**:
  - *Positive Advocacy*: Delighted customers brand advocates ban jate hain, jisse prospective buyers ka risk perception kam ho jata hai.
  - *Negative Advocacy*: Detractors negative reviews failate hain. Connective technologies is negative feedback ko amplify ("word-of-mouse") kar deti hain.

## Unit 12: International Trade in Services

### 1. General Agreement on Trade in Services (GATS) — 4 Modes of Supply
* **Mode 1: Cross-Border Supply**: Service border cross karti hai, lekin provider aur consumer apne-apne desh me hi rehte hain.
  - *Example*: Indian developer ka Germany ke client ke liye software code email karna, ya Indian doctor ka US patient ko teleconsultation dena.
* **Mode 2: Consumption Abroad**: Consumer service consume karne ke liye provider ke desh me travel karta hai.
  - *Example*: US citizen ka heart surgery ke liye India aana (medical tourism), ya foreign student ka Indian university me padhna.
* **Mode 3: Commercial Presence**: Service firm doosre desh me apni branch ya subsidiary open karti hai.
  - *Example*: State Bank of India ka London me branch open karna, ya Indian hospital group ka Mauritius me clinic chalana.
* **Mode 4: Presence of Natural Persons**: Ek individual doosre desh me temporarily travel karta hai service deliver karne ke liye.
  - *Example*: Indian IT consultant ka client ke office me software install karne ke liye H-1B visa par New York jana.

### 2. India's Opportunities & Constraints in International Services
* **Opportunities (Moke)**:
  * *Software & IT Hub*: India global sourcing market ka ~55% control karta hai aur 75% digital talent India me hai (Mode 1 & Mode 4 exports).
  * *Telemedicine & Medical Tourism*: Kam cost me world-class treatment, jisse Mode 2 exports badh rahe hain.
  * *Skilled Manpower Pool*: English-speaking engineers, doctors aur accountants ki bharmar jo price-quality advantage deti hai.
* **Constraints (Badhayein)**:
  * *Market Access Barriers*: Western countries me visa restrictions, entry quotas aur strict work permit rules (Mode 4 limits).
  * *Mutual Recognition Agreements (MRAs) ki kami*: Indian qualifications (law, accountancy, medicine) foreign countries me directly recognized nahi hoti.
  * *Domestic Regulations*: Accounting aur legal services me high Services Trade Restrictiveness Index (STRI) score.
  * *GSP Benefits ka hatna*: Western markets me preferential tariff benefits ka end hona.

## Unit 13: Managing Demand and Capacity

### 1. Demand-Capacity Mismatch & Implications (Mismatch aur uske Implications)
Services perishable hoti hain aur unhe store nahi kiya ja sakta, isliye demand aur supply match karna bada challenge hai:
* **Implications**:
  * *Excess Demand (Demand > Max Capacity)*: Customers wapas chale jate hain (lost revenue), resources overstretch hote hain, aur service quality girti hai.
  * *Demand > Optimum Capacity*: Kisi ko mana nahi kiya jata, par facilities overcrowded ho jati hain, staff thak jata hai, aur customer satisfaction drop hota hai.
  * *Excess Capacity (Demand < Optimum Capacity)*: Fixed assets aur employees bekaar baithe rehte hain (underutilization), jisse unit cost badhta hai aur dry atmosphere hota hai.

### 2. Strategies for Matching Capacity and Demand
* **Shifting Demand to Fit Capacity (Demand Management)**:
  * *Pricing*: Off-peak hours me low prices rakhna (e.g., late-night flight discounts, restaurant Happy Hours).
  * *Product*: Off-peak seasonal packages banana (e.g., business hotels ka weekends par family entertainment packages dena).
  * *Place*: Delivery timings badalna ya satellite clinics open karna.
  * *Promotion*: Non-peak hours ke advantages promote karna (e.g., mid-week shopping incentives).
* **Adjusting Capacity to Fit Demand (Capacity Management)**:
  * *Part-Time Staff*: Peak seasons me temporary employees hire karna.
  * *Overtime*: Apne employees ko peak times par extra shifts dena.
  * *Cross-Training*: Staff ko multiple tasks sikhana (e.g., gates par help karne ke liye ticketing staff ko cross-train karna).
  * *Scheduling*: Maintenance aur staff training ko off-peak seasons me schedule karna.
  * *Customer Participation*: Self-service promote karna (e.g., ATMs, self-checkout kiosks).

### 3. Yield Management (Revenue Management)
* **Definition**: Real-time demand forecasting ke base par price adjust karke fixed, perishable capacity se maximum revenue/profit generate karne ki technique.
* **Ideal Conditions**:
  1. Relatively fixed capacity (e.g., hotel rooms, flight seats).
  2. Segmented market (business vs. leisure travel).
  3. Perishable inventory (khali seat ka revenue dobara nahi mil sakta).
  4. Advance booking systems.
  5. Fluctuating demand patterns.
  6. Low marginal sales cost par high capacity-change cost.
* **Managerial Risks**:
  * *Customer Alienation*: Jab customer ko pata chale ki doosre ne same service ke liye kam price pay kiya hai.
  * *Loss of Competitive Focus*: Zyaada yield ke chakkar me service quality ignore ho jana.
  * *Employee Morale*: Staff ko lagta hai ki automated software unki judgment ko bypass kar raha hai.

### 4. Service Guarantees
* **Definition**: Ek formal promise jisme service failure hone par refund, credit, ya compensation dene ki guarantee hoti hai.
* **Four Features of an Effective Guarantee (Hart ke 4 Parameters)**:
  1. *Unconditional*: Koi hidden terms ya "ifs/buts" na ho.
  2. *Easy to Understand & Communicate*: Simple aur memorable wording.
  3. *Meaningful*: Aisi cheez guarantee karna jisse customer ko sach me farq pade aur payout ka value achha ho.
  4. *Easy to Invoke & Collect*: Claim process me koi red tape ya jhanjhat na ho.
* **Benefits**: Customer risk kam karta hai, trust aur purchase intent badhata hai, quality standards align karta hai, aur instant customer feedback deta hai.
* **Customer Abuse Prevention**: Claims ko verify karna, repeat claimants ko database me track karna, aur staff ko fake claims identify karne ki training dena.

## Unit 14: Emerging Issues in Services Marketing

### 1. Customer Relationship Management (CRM) & Retention
* **CRM**: Ek database system jo multiple touchpoints (websites, social media, transactions) se customer data collect karke unhe customized aur personalized experience deta hai.
* **Benefits**: Customization support, automation of support tasks, aur trends and insights report karna.
* **Customer Retention Logic**:
  - *Zero Defections*: Customer defection rate 5% kam karne se profits 100% tak badh sakte hain (Reichheld & Sasser), kyunki:
    * Naya customer lane ka high acquisition cost nahi hota.
    * Purana customer zyaada buy karta hai, operational costs kam hoti hain (kyunki wo system samajhta hai), premium price pay karne ko ready hota hai, aur referral word-of-mouth deta hai.

### 2. Self-Service Technologies (SSTs)
* **Concept**: Aisi technologies jinke through customers bina kisi employee ke direct involvement ke khud service produce aur consume kar sakte hain (e.g., ATMs, train booking apps, self-checkout counters).
* **SST Evaluation**:
  | Benefits (Fayde) | Drawbacks (Nuksaan) |
  | :--- | :--- |
  | **Convenience**: 24/7 availability. | **Equipment Failures**: Machine kharab hone par customer frustration. |
  | **Speed**: Waiting queues khatam hoti hain. | **Lack of Human Contact**: Split payment ya custom queries solve nahi ho pate. |
  | **Accuracy**: Customer khud specs input karta hai. | **High Setup Costs**: Initial hardware/software setup aur maintenance expense. |
  | **Personalization**: Data-driven customized UI. | **Complexity**: Technologically challenged logon ke liye complex. |

### 3. Key Emerging Trends in Connected Services Marketing
* **Horizontal Innovation & Co-Creation**: Companies internal R&D ke bajaye customers ke sath milkar design karti hain (co-creation) aur competitors ke sath collaborate karti hain (co-opetition). E.g., P&G Connect + Develop.
* **The F-Factor (Friends, Families, Fans, Followers)**: Customers ab ads ya expert opinion se zyaada apne social media circles aur TripAdvisor/Zomato reviews par trust karte hain.
* **Social Conformity**: Sawaal-jawab aur reviews sharing ke badhne se, ab personal purchases social decisions ban rahi hain.
* **Customer Path Transition (4 A's to 5 A's)**:
  - *Traditional 4 A's*: Aware $\rightarrow$ Attitude $\rightarrow$ Act $\rightarrow$ Act Again. (Funnel driven by ads).
  - *Connected 5 A's (Kotler)*: **Aware** (exposures) $\rightarrow$ **Appeal** (attraction to short list) $\rightarrow$ **Ask** (social search, reviews check) $\rightarrow$ **Act** (purchase & usage) $\rightarrow$ **Advocate** (spontaneous advocacy/recommendation).
* **The O Zone (O3 Influence Framework)**:
  - Marketers ko customer path ke cross teen tarah ke influences use karne chahiye:
    1. **Outer Influence**: Company ke ads, PR campaigns aur sales representatives.
    2. **Others' Influence**: Word-of-mouth jo social circle aur online community se milta hai (*Ask* stage me sabse critical).
    3. **Own Influence**: Customer ka apna experience aur usage history (*Act* aur *Advocate* stages me sabse critical).
