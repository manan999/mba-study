### UNIT 4: COMMUNICATION SKILLS

#### 1. Objectives of Communication in Sales (Sales me Communication ke Objectives)
*   Har sales call persuasive communication ki ek exercise hoti hai. Iske key objectives niche diye gaye hain:
    1.  *Laying Credibility:* Expertise (product knowledge) aur relationship (trustworthiness) ke through customer ka trust/vishwas jeetna.
    2.  *Framing Goals:* Ek common ground dhoondna aur product ko is tarah position karna ki woh customer ki specific problem ko solve kare.
    3.  *Compelling Evidence:* Apni presentation ko memorable banane ke liye clear language, stories, metaphors aur numbers ka use karna.
    4.  *Emotional Connection:* Product ke liye passion dikhana aur buyer ki inner feelings ke saath connect karna.
*   **Active Listening:** Sales communication ka sabse bada stone (cornerstone). Active listening ka matlab sirf response dene ke liye nahi balki customer ko samajhne ke liye sunna hai. Isme paraphrasing karna, pause lena aur clear questions puchna shamil hai.

#### 2. Verbal vs. Written Communication in Selling (Verbal aur Written me Antar)
*   **Differences:**
| Feature | Written Communication | Verbal/Oral Communication |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Purpose** | Analytical & Reasoning (complex data ke liye) | Assimilative & Emotive (rapport/relation banane ke liye) |
| **Audience** | Non-Interactive (one-way message hota hai) | Interactive (two-way dialogue/baatcheet) |
| **Medium** | Frozen (text, brochures, letters) | Fluid (voice tone, words, expressions) |
| **Feedback** | Delayed (der se) ya bilkul nahi | Quick & Immediate (turant) |
| **Flexibility** | Rigid, permanent record hota hai | Highly flexible aur on the spot badla ja sakta hai |
| **Cost** | High (printing, drafting cost) | Low (direct conversation) |

*   *Written Application:* Sales proposals, brochures aur quotation letters brief (chote), focused aur data-backed hone chahiye.
*   *Verbal Application:* Real-time product pitch, objections ko handle karna aur customer interaction.

#### 3. Relevance of Non-Verbal Communication (Non-Verbal ka Importance)
*   **Impact:** Research ke mutabik, verbal communication ke total impact ka **70% se zyada part** non-verbal cues (body language, tone, gestures, physical space) se aata hai.
*   **Key Non-Verbal Cues:**
    *   *Posture (Baithne/Khade hone ka tarika):* Sidhe baithna professionalism aur interest show karta hai; jhuk kar baithna ek casual aur uncaring attitude dikhata hai.
    *   *Eye Contact:* Trust build karta hai. Customer ki taraf ghurne (staring) se bachein aur idhar-udhar dekhne se bhi bachein kyunki isse dishonesty lagti hai.
    *   *Voice Modulation:* Pitch, speed aur volume se customer ka confidence badhta hai. Dhire bolne se control show hota hai aur bohot fast bolne se lagta hai ki aap pushy hain.
    *   *Proxemics:* Customer ke saath ek appropriate physical distance (personal space) maintain karna taaki use crowded ya uncomfortable na lage.

#### 4. Telecommunication and Remote Communication in Modern Sales
*   **Telecommunication (Phone Etiquette):**
    *   Call karne se pehle content check aur ready rakhein; use short aur crisp rakhein.
    *   Receiver ki identity turant verify karein ("Kya main Mr. X se baat kar raha hoon?").
    *   Reason bataye bina customer ko hold par na dalein.
    *   Call ko achhe terms me end karein (e.g., "Aap se baat karke achha laga"); abruptly call cut na karein.
*   **Remote Communication (Virtual Sales):**
    *   *Synchronous:* Real-time video meetings (Zoom, MS Teams). Iske liye professional background, clean audio/video feed aur alert posture chahiye.
    *   *Asynchronous:* Pre-recorded demo videos ya follow-up emails jo baad me dekhne ke liye share kiye jaate hain.

### UNIT 5: NEGOTIATION SKILLS

#### 1. Defining Negotiation and Distinguishing it from Selling
*   **Negotiation (Mol-Tol):** Conflicts ko resolve karne ki ek process jahan do ya do se zyada parties ek mutually acceptable compromise tak pahunchne ke liye apni demands ko modify karti hain.
*   **Selling vs. Negotiating (Bechna vs. Mol-tol me Antar):**
    *   *Selling:* Customer ki needs ko identify karna aur unhe product ya service lene ke liye convince/persuade karna.
    *   *Negotiating:* Jab buyer interest show kar de, toh transaction ke terms (price, delivery date, credit terms) par agree karna. Selling desire build karti hai; negotiating transaction ke terms ko settle karti hai.

#### 2. The Negotiating Continuum (Negotiating Range)
*   Negotiation me dono parties apne ideal positions se chalte hue ek common agreement point ki taraf badhti hain.
```
Seller's Ideal (Max Price) ◄──────────────────────► Customer's Ideal (Min Price)
                                 ▲
                           Settlement Point
                          (Mutually Agreeable)
```
*   Negotiators ke skills aur relative bargaining power par depend karta hai ki settlement point kis taraf bend hoga.

#### 3. Steps of the Negotiation Process (Negotiation ke Steps)
1.  **Prepare (Taiyari):** Apne objectives decide aur prioritize karein. Buyer ke attitude ki details nikalen aur flexible strategy banayein.
2.  **Discussion (Charcha):** Needs aur limits ko samjhein. Interruption, sarcasm ya bohot zyada bolne se bachein.
3.  **Signaling (Ishaare):** Apni ideal position se thoda piche hatne ke hints dena. Opponent ke signals ko dhyan se sunna.
4.  **Proposing (Prastav):** Temporary proposals banana. Inhe conditional rakhein ("Agar aap 100 units buy karenge, tabhi hum 5% discount de payenge").
5.  **Packaging the Offer:** Price, delivery, warranty aur payment terms ko ek sath bundle karke customer ke samne pesh karna.
6.  **Bargaining:** Concessions (chhoot) ka exchange. *Kabhi bhi koi chhoot bina badle me kuch maange mat dein.*
7.  **Closing and Agreeing (Deal Close Karna):** Concessions ko rokne ka final point. Agreement ko turant details me document karein.
8.  **Handling Deadlock (Rukawat):** Agar negotiation ruk jaye, toh negotiator change karein, break/recess lein, ya problem ko chote parts me divide karein.

#### 4. Negotiation Strategies
*   **Strike Back:** Buyer ke pressure ka aggressively response dena. Yeh relationships kharab kar sakta hai, isliye dhyan se use karein.
*   **Give In (Haar Maanna):** Deal close karne ke liye customer ki saari demands maan lena. Isse profit margins kharab hote hain aur galat precedent set hota hai.
*   **Break Off (Chod Dena):** Agar terms bilkul unacceptable hon toh negotiation chhod dena. Yeh aapki position ko validate karta hai aur loss-making deals se bachata hai.

#### 5. Principles of Sales Negotiations
*   Sab kuch conditional hona chahiye ("Agar aap... tab hum...").
*   Poore bundle/package ko dhyan me rakhein; single items par alag se mol-tol na karein.
*   Concessions ke liye jagah rakhne ke liye shuruat high but realistic opening position se karein.
*   Bolne se zyada sunne par focus karein; information hi power hai.

### UNIT 6: MERCHANDISING AND MANAGING SALES DISPLAYS

#### 1. Merchandising and Visual Merchandising (VM)
*   **Merchandising:** Retail level par sales badhane ke liye products ko display aur present karna.
*   **Visual Merchandising:** Retail store space, product layout aur display materials ko is tarah design karna taaki customers ka dhyan khiche, unhe educate kare aur buy karne ke liye motivate kare.
*   **VM me Salesperson ka Role:**
    *   *Silent Salesperson:* Ek accha display customers ko inform aur guide karta hai, jisse sales staff ki requirement kam ho jati hai.
    *   *Boomerang Effect se Bachna:* Zyada aggressive sales staff customers ko irritate kar sakte hain aur unhe bhaga sakte hain. Accha display customer ko visual cues ke sath akele browse karne ki freedom deta hai.
    *   *Feedback Provider:* Salespeople dekhte hain ki customer store me kaise navigate kar rahe hain aur VM layout change karne ka feedback dete hain.

#### 2. Store Design Basics (Store Design ke Basics)
*   **Exterior Design (Storefront):** Pehla impression (signboard, entrance, window display). Iska main target raste se guzarne walo ko store ke andar bulana hai.
*   **Interior Design (Andaruni Design):** Floor layout, light setting, fixtures aur billing counters. Isse customer ka flow poore store me smoothly rotate hona chahiye.

#### 3. Key Design Principles of Sales Displays (Display ke Principles)
*   **Unity (Ekta):** Saare elements (signs, products, props) organized dikhne chahiye aur ek single concept ki tarah kaam karne chahiye taaki clutter (kachra) na lage.
*   **Balance (Santulan):**
    *   *Symmetrical (Formal):* Center line ke dono taraf equal visual weight hona (stable aur clean lagta hai).
    *   *Asymmetrical (Informal):* Alg-alag elements lekin equal visual weight hona (dynamic aur interesting lagta hai).
*   **Contrast:** Kisi product ko contrast colors, sizes ya light settings ke through highlight karna (e.g., dark background par light product rakhna).
*   **Emphasis / Dominance:** Kisi badi item, color block, ya special effect ka use karke customer ka dhyan direct ek specific place par khichna.
*   **Repetition:** Pattern ya colors ko repeat karna taaki memory recall bade aur background sundar lage.
*   **Proportion:** Visual harmony jahan elements ka size store environment ke size ke according normal dikhe.
*   **Color Blocking:** Products ko color ke according line up karna (e.g., red/yellow aur cool blue/green color sections banana) taaki shopper ki eye smoothly guide ho.

#### 4. Types of Displays (with Examples)
*   **Assortment Display:**
    *   *Open:* Customer products ko touch aur try kar sakte hain (e.g., Crossword me books, Shoppers Stop me kapde).
    *   *Closed:* Items glass panels ke piche surakshit hoti hain (e.g., jewelry shops ya expensive Titan watches).
*   **Theme Display:** Kisi festival ya season par target display (e.g., Diwali lights display, shaadi ka mandap theme saree shops me, Christmas decoration).
*   **Coordinated/Ensemble Display (Matching Display):** Related products ko ek sath display karna taaki suggestive selling ho sake (e.g., mannequin ko matching shirt, pant, tie, shoes aur bag pehnana).
*   **Unit Display:** Ek jaise items ko structured line me rakhna (e.g., cups ya spoons ki regular line).
*   **Classification Dominance:** Product line ki wide variety aur depth ko walls par vertically display karna.
*   **Dump Bins / Cut Cases:** Bins me loose items bechne ke liye rakhna taaki low-price lag sake (supermarkets jaise D-Mart me common hai).
