### Unit 7: Recruitment, Selection and Training of the Sales Force

#### 1. Sales Job Analysis & Job Description (Job Analysis aur Job Description)
*   **Sales Job Analysis:** Kisi sales position ke baare me systematically saari information collect karna taaki uske tasks, duties aur working environment conditions ka pata lagaya ja sake.
*   **Job Description:** Job analysis se derived ek formal document jo define karta hai:
    *   **Tasks & Duties:** Kaun se specific kaam karne honge (e.g., travel planning karna, sales presentations dena, complaints check karna).
    *   **Performance Standards (Kaam ka Standard):** Expected volume aur quality levels.
    *   **KSA Matrix:** Role ke liye zaroori **Knowledge** (product, competitor, terrain), **Skills** (communication, closing, routing) aur **Attitudes** (empathy, self-discipline, energy).

#### 2. Recruitment Sources: Traditional vs. Contemporary (Recruitment ke Sources)
Recruitment qualified candidates ka ek potential pool create karne ki process hai.

| Feature / Source | Traditional Sources (Purane Sources) | Contemporary Sources (Naye/Modern Sources) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Examples** | Employment agencies, akhbaar ke ads (newspaper advertisements), walk-ins, company ke andhar ke transfers, customer referrals. | Online job portals (Naukri, Monster), professional networks (LinkedIn), social media recruiting, AI screening tools, employee referral networks. |
| **Pros** | Local reach hoti hai, log format ko achhe se samajhte hain, agencies screening karke deti hain. | Pure desh/world me reach, bohot fast hai, cost-effective hai, passive candidates tak access milta hai. |
| **Cons** | Reach limited hoti hai, response slow hota hai, print ads me bohot kharcha aata hai. | Resume spamming hoti hai (faltu resume aate hain), digital filters chahiye hote hain, expectations mismatch ho sakti hai. |

*   **Technical/Engineering Trainees ke liye choice (Kya recommend karenge?):**
    1.  **Campus Recruitment (Engineering Colleges):** Colleges se direct fresh, trainable aur technical knowledge wale students mil jaate hain.
    2.  **Specialized Online Portals / LinkedIn:** Technical keywords ke basis par pre-screened profiles ko select karne ke liye.

#### 3. Selection Process & Interviewing Tools (Selection ke Steps aur Interviews)
*   **Selection Process ke Steps:**
    ```
    Screening Applications ➔ Testing (Aptitude/Personality) ➔ Personal Interviews ➔ Reference Checks ➔ Physical/Medical Exam ➔ Final Offer
    ```
*   **Interviewing Techniques (Interviews ke Types):**
    *   **Structured Interviews:** Highly standardized hote hain. Har candidate se ek jaise pehle se decide kiye gaye sawal puche jaate hain.
        *   *Pros:* Personal bias (bhedbhav) khatam hota hai, reliable hai, candidates ko compare karna easy hai.
        *   *Cons:* Flexibility nahi hoti, candidate ke unique background details ko depth me investigate nahi kar sakte.
    *   **Unstructured Interviews:** Open-ended aur conversational hote hain jahan sawal candidate ke answers ke according generate hote hain.
    *   **Behavioral / Situational Interviews:** Purani performances check karne ke sawal ("Mujhe aisi situation ke baare me batao jab...") ya hypothetical situations ("Agar aisa ho jaye toh aap kya karenge...").
*   **Psychological/Personality Tests:**
    *   **MBTI (Myers-Briggs Type Indicator):** Candidate ko 4 dimensions par check karta hai: Introversion vs. Extroversion (I/E), Sensing vs. Intuition (S/N), Thinking vs. Feeling (T/F) aur Judging vs. Perceiving (J/P). Isse team fit aur selling style (jaise relationship selling vs hard selling) ka pata chalta hai.
    *   **Wonderlic Cognitive Test:** Speed aur logic testing test hai jo time limit ke under decision-making test karta hai.

#### 4. Sales Training Process: The ACMEE Approach (Training ka Framework)
Ek accha sales training program **ACMEE** framework follow karta hai:

```
[ Aims ] ➔ [ Content ] ➔ [ Methods ] ➔ [ Execution ] ➔ [ Evaluation ] ➔ (Feedback & Redesign)
```

1.  **Aims (Maksad):** Clear, measurable objectives set karna (e.g., conversion rate 15% badhana, new product specs seekhna).
2.  **Content (Subject Matter):** Kya kya sikhana hai:
    *   Company ka history, policy aur structures.
    *   Product knowledge aur applications.
    *   Sales skills (prospecting, objection handling, negotiation).
    *   Reporting system aur tools.
3.  **Methods (Tarike):** Training kaise di jayegi:
    *   *Self-Learning Modules:* Online packages self-study ke liye.
    *   *Classroom Training:* Lectures, role-plays, cases (off-the-job).
    *   *On-the-Job Training (OJT):* Senior manager ke sath real field me jaakar seekhna.
4.  **Execution:** Logistics manage karna, calendar schedule karna, trainers select karna aur sessions conduct karna.
5.  **Evaluation (Moolyankan):** Performance tests aur field feedback ke through check karna ki training kitni successful rahi. Gaps hone par cycle repeat kiya jata hai.

#### 5. Identifying Training Needs (Training Needs Kaise Pehchanein)
**Training Need** tab arise hoti hai jab actual knowledge/skill/attitude aur standard expected knowledge/skill/attitude ke beech gap ho.
*   **Needs Identification ki Process:**
    1.  Job aur expected performance standards ko define karna (Job Description).
    2.  Requirements ke basis par zaroori KSAs list down karna.
    3.  Sales force ki current performance ko appraise karna taaki deviations ka pata chale.
    4.  Training gaps identify karna (Expected vs. Actual).
    5.  External changes (naye products, competitors' strategy) ke according future needs assess karna.
    6.  Needs ko categorize aur prioritize karna immediate, mid-term aur long-term targets ke liye.
*   **Needs pata karne ke Methods:**
    *   *Self-Observation:* Managers reps ko directly field visit me observe karte hain.
    *   *Field & Sales Reports:* Conversion rates, average call ratios aur data reports analyze karna.
    *   *Surveys & Questionnaires:* Reps ya unke managers se direct feedback lena.
    *   *Face-to-Face Interviews:* Reps ke problems ko samajhne ke liye personal discussions.
    *   *Focus Groups:* Group discussions ke through common problems identify karna.

#### 6. Learning Styles (Sikhne ke Tarike)
*   **Activists:** Kuch naya karke seekhte hain (role-plays, interactive sessions chahiye).
*   **Reflectors:** Dekh kar aur observe karke seekhte hain (demonstrations aur reviews pasand karte hain).
*   **Theorists:** Logic, models aur concepts ke through seekhte hain.
*   **Pragmatists:** Real-world case study aur practical tips ke through seekhte hain.

#### 7. Segmented Training Program Designs (Examples)
*   **Textile Machinery (Technical B2B):** Isme product engineering details, industrial buying behavior, long selling cycle, ROI models aur intensive on-the-job training par main focus hota hai.
*   **Senior Salespersons for Management Roles:** Leadership skills, budgeting, forecasting, coaching, conflict resolution aur strategy planning par focus hota hai.
*   **Pharma Sales (Medical Reps):** Medical drugs pharmacology details, short doctor presentation skills (detailing), doctors ke doubts door karna, clinical reports share karna aur ethical standards maintain karna.

### Unit 8: Compensation Management

#### 1. Components of Sales Compensation (Compensation ke Parts)
*   **Direct Compensation:** Salary, commission, bonus aur allowances.
*   **Indirect Compensation:** Insurance benefits, holiday trips, pension plans, recognition schemes.
*   **Monetary vs. Non-Monetary Rewards:**
    *   *Monetary:* Hard cash aur financial items.
    *   *Non-Monetary:* Trophy, certificate, promotion title ("Salesperson of the Month"), public appreciation.

#### 2. Direct Financial Compensation Plans
| Compensation Plan | Description | Advantages (Fayde) | Disadvantages (Nuksan) | Best Suited For (Kahan suited hai) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Straight Salary (Fix Salary)** | Ek fixed amount har month pay kiya jata hai bina sales volume check kiye. | • Income ki suraksha.<br>• Administration simple hai.<br>• Territory changes par resistance nahi hota.<br>• Cost control predictable hai. | • Koi financial motivation nahi hai extra work ke liye.<br>• Performers demotivate ho sakte hain.<br>• Sales drop hone par bhi fix cost lagti hai. | • Missionary selling (direct order generation nahi).<br>• Team sales.<br>• Customer/technical support positions. |
| **Straight Commission (Sirf Commission)** | Payout strictly sales value ya margin ke percentage par depend karta hai. | • Maximum motivation aur performance incentive.<br>• Selling costs direct profit proportional hain.<br>• Stars ko attract karta hai. | • Income ki insecurity rehti hai.<br>• Company loyalty nahi hoti.<br>• Reps administrative/non-selling tasks nahi karte.<br>• Territory split par resistance. | • Transactional/Hard selling (Life insurance, real estate, direct selling agents). |
| **Combination Plan (Salary + Incentive)** | Fix basic salary ke sath variable commission ya bonus ka mix. | • Security aur incentive dono milte hain.<br>• Company control aur flexibility banaye rakhta hai. | • Design aur execution complex hai.<br>• Sometime high achievers limit feel karte hain. | • FMCG, Consumer Durables, aur B2B industrial products. |

*   **Drawing Accounts:** Ek hybrid model jahan salesperson ko commission ke against advance cash pay kiya jata hai ("draw"). Agar commission draw se kam rehta hai, toh bacha hua cash agle cycle me carry-forward debt ban jata hai.

#### 3. Factors Influencing Compensation Plan Design
1.  **Product Life Cycle (PLC) Stages:**
    *   *Introduction:* High fixed salary support (customer awareness aur educational call support ke liye).
    *   *Growth:* High commission percentage (volume acquisition aur aggressive push badhane ke liye).
    *   *Maturity:* Indirect benefits, retail push supports aur team bonuses (relationship maintenance ke liye).
    *   *Decline:* Specialized targeted bonuses (purana stock nikalne ke liye).
2.  **Salesforce Demographics:** Age aur family profile preferences ko influence karti hai. Young reps high commission chahte hain jabki seniors safety aur health benefits ko priority dete hain.
3.  **Marketing Strategy:** Agar target revenue badhana hai toh volume incentive; agar profitability target hai toh commission margins par tie kiya jata hai.
4.  **Competitors' Packages:** Industry trends ke sath compensation package match karna taaki high attrition (sales staff turnover) na ho.

#### 4. Criteria for a Sound Sales Compensation Plan (Ache Plan ke Features)
*   **Simple & Understandable:** Rep khud apni income calculate kar sake.
*   **Fair & Equitable:** Mehnat aur performance ke level par reward proportionate ho.
*   **Flexible:** Market, seasons aur products ke changes ke dynamic update ho sake.
*   **Cost Control:** Company budgets ke limits me sales cost control rahe.
*   **Balanced:** Volume aur relationship building (qualitative efforts) dono ko support kare.

#### 5. Why Compensation Plans Fail (Failure ke Reasons)
*   **Strategic Misalignment:** Company ka objective margin badhana hai par incentive volume par hai.
*   **Mismatch with Job Design:** Industrial sales me group work chahiye par company individual performance pay kar rahi hai.
*   **Inadequate Performance Metrics:** Bohot complex ya unrealistic targets rakhna jisse reps confuse ho jayein.
*   **Escalating Selling Costs:** Sales ke comparison me incentive payouts bohot high hona.
*   **Talent Turnover:** Achha payout na milne ki wajah se top performers ka leave kar jana.

#### 6. Sales Compensation Scenarios (Analysis)
*   **Life Insurance Salesperson:** Commission-heavy package sahi hai kyunki cold calls aur self-prospecting requirements high hoti hain.
*   **Computer Hardware Salesperson:** Fix salary + moderate commission margins par. B2B deal closing me mahino lagte hain aur support dynamic chahiye hota hai.
*   **AE (TV Commercial Space):** Salary + gross margin commission. High-value clients ke sath balance maintain karna zaroori hai.
*   **Multi-Tier Plan driving volume but losing money (Case Study):**
    *   *Diagnosis:* Incentives sales *volume* par calculate ho rahe hain, margins par nahi. Reps target hit karne ke liye huge discount offers de rahe hain.
    *   *CFO Alternative:* Commission aur bonus payout structures ko **Gross Margin** par shift karein aur discounting powers par threshold ceiling lagayein.

### Unit 9: Sales Leadership: Motivation, Coaching and Counseling

#### 1. Sales Leadership vs. Sales Management (Leadership vs Management)
*   **Sales Management:** Planning, budgeting, organizing, auditing aur administrative controls par focus karta hai. (Doing things right - kaam ko sahi dhang se karna).
*   **Sales Leadership:** Guide karna, influence karna, inspire karna, coach karna aur path show karna. (Doing the right things - sahi kaam karna).

#### 2. Situational Sales Leadership Styles (Leadership Styles)
Leadership behavior rep ke competency/motivation levels ke basis par modify kiya jata hai (Task vs. Relationship Behavior):
*   *Style 1 (Tells):* High Task, Low Relationship. Inexperienced aur new reps ke liye jahan clear directions chahiye.
*   *Style 2 (Persuades):* High Task, High Relationship. Un reps ke liye jinki performance average hai par improvement potential hai.
*   *Style 3 (Participates):* Low Task, High Relationship. Competent par demotivated ya frustrated staff ke liye.
*   *Style 4 (Delegates):* Low Task, Low Relationship. Star performers aur senior highly self-motivated sales reps ke liye.

#### 3. Power Sources in Sales Leadership (Power ke Sources)
*   **Legitimate Power:** Company structure me manager ki post ke rights.
*   **Reward Power:** Bonus aur benefits distribute karne ki capacity.
*   **Coercive Power:** Punishment, territory changes, warnings dene ke rights.
*   **Expert Power:** Product, client aur market ke direct expert knowledge se mili izzat.
*   **Referent Power:** Charisma aur manager ke strong attitude ki wajah se rep ka unhe emulate/copy karne ka desire.

#### 4. The Pygmalion Effect (Expectations ki Power)
*   **Definition:** Higher expectations se performance ka badhna aur low expectations se performance ka crash hona.
*   **Sales Context:** Agar manager pehle se assume karega ki rep fail hoga, toh unke dynamics badal jaate hain (e.g., call ignore karna, validation na dena). Isse rep ka self-esteem crash ho jata hai, woh active calls kam karta hai aur end me result fail aata hai (expectations prove ho jati hain). Positive managers hamesha high support levels maintain karte hain.

#### 5. Salesforce Motivation: Maslow's Need Hierarchy Applied
*   **Self-Actualization Needs:** Career growth promotions, advanced master training modules.
*   **Esteem Needs:** "Salesperson of the Month" tags, certificates, status trophies.
*   **Social Needs:** Team outings, review meet lunches, direct coordination groups.
*   **Safety & Security Needs:** Fixed safety allowance packages, medical insurance, basic job stability.
*   **Physiological Needs:** Basic basic salary jo food, shelter aur survival ensure kare.
*   *Low-Cost Motivators:* Public recognition, autonomy in call schedules, direct feedback appreciation notes.

#### 6. Sales Coaching vs. Induction Training
*   **Sales Coaching:** Ongoing, individualized process jisme Joint Sales Call (field visit) me rep ko real-world behaviors par guidance diya jata hai.

| Dimension | Induction Training | Sales Coaching |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Timing** | Sirf onboarding time ya specific batch intervals. | Continuous (weekly ya daily schedule). |
| **Setting** | Classroom setting ya corporate office. | On-the-job real customer doors. |
| **Scope** | Poore group ke liye common modules. | One-on-one personal guidelines. |
| **Focus** | Product detail aur company guidelines sikhana. | Behaviors aur closing techniques correct karna. |

### Unit 10: Evaluation of Sales Force and Monitoring

#### 1. Need and Purpose of Monitoring & Evaluation (Appraisal aur Control)
*   **Closes the Loop:** Targets (budgets/forecasts) aur actual results ko cross-check karna.
*   **Tracks Activities:** Door-to-door ya remote locations par chal rahi field activities ko monitor karna.
*   **Identifies Gaps:** Gaps and training needs spotlight karna behavior analysis ke through.
*   **Maintains Accountability:** Performance ke direct ratio me incentives process streamline karna.

#### 2. Quantitative vs. Qualitative Evaluation Criteria
*   **Quantitative Criteria (Objective):** Statistical indicators.
    *   *Output metrics:* Total sales volume, gross margins, new accounts opened, order count.
    *   *Input metrics:* Daily calls, travel logs, customer presentations, actual sales expenses.
*   **Qualitative Criteria (Subjective):** Behavioral checks.
    *   *Skills:* Communication details, handling objection style, negotiation checks.
    *   *Self-Organization:* Routing layout planning, database maintain records, regular reports submission.
    *   *Attitudes:* Self-motivation, integrity levels, cooperation standards.

#### 3. Key Performance Ratios
*   **Strike Rate / Conversion Rate:**
    $$\text{Strike Rate} = \frac{\text{Number of Orders}}{\text{Number of Quotations Submitted}} \times 100$$
*   **Average Order Value:**
    $$\text{Average Order Value} = \frac{\text{Sales Revenue}}{\text{Number of Orders}}$$
*   **Sales per Call Ratio:**
    $$\text{Sales per Call} = \frac{\text{Sales Revenue}}{\text{Number of Calls Made}}$$
*   **Expense-to-Sales Ratio:**
    $$\text{Expense-to-Sales} = \frac{\text{Selling Expenses}}{\text{Sales Revenue}} \times 100$$

#### 4. Sales Reporting Systems (Key Reports)
1.  **Activity / Progress Reports:** Daily logs jo calls, meetings aur client status list down karte hain.
2.  **Expense Reports:** Travel vouchers claims logs for transparency.
3.  **Work Plans / Call Schedules:** Pehle se plan kiya gaya route chart, jisse management reps ki coordination simplify kar sake.
4.  **Lost Business Reports:** Competitors ke paas chale gaye accounts ki feedback report taaki weak links find ho sakein.
5.  **Complaint Reports:** Customer disputes aur resolution timelines ka log.

#### 5. Issues in Sales Appraisal Systems (Appraisal ke Problems)
*   *Qualitative Bias:* Manager ke personal opinions se scores bias hona.
*   *Person-to-Person Comparison:* Alag territories (e.g., different competitor density) wale reps ko direct linear comparison me judge karna.
*   *Arbitrary Targets:* Bina statistics ke targets set karna.

#### 6. Sales Appraisal Scenario (Case Study Analysis)
*   **Strictly Quarterly Volume Rankings:**
    *   *Drawbacks:* Reps margin drop karke volume load karenge dealers par, customer relationships ko ignore karenge, aur low potential zones ke staff systematically negative ranks par aayenge.
    *   *Better Design:* **Composite Score Card** use karein: Sales Volume (30%), Gross Margin (20%), Customer Satisfaction index (20%), New Accounts count (15%) aur Qualitative Supervisor Score (15%).
